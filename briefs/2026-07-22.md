## 🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-07-22
**Read time: ~4 minutes**

### ⚡ Today's Top Signal
Section 301 tariffs have reached their final countdown: Brazil's 25% forced-labor duty activated this morning (July 22), and the broader 10–12.5% on 60+ countries implements in 48 hours (July 24). This is the most consequential tariff transition of the year — replacing the SCOTUS-voided IEEPA rates with no grandfathering, no rate cap, and no expiry date. The immediate freight signal is a compliance-induced demand pause: load posts fell 9% WoW this week as shippers froze bookings awaiting clarity. For Uber Freight, the strategic question is two-sided: the brokerage must navigate a Q3 demand trough as shipper planning halts, while the 4PL/managed transportation team has a concrete window to demonstrate AI-driven network replanning value before the August import surge — which NRF projects will hit a record 2.47M TEU — hits domestic truckload networks.

### 📋 Regulatory Watch
- **Section 301 tariffs implementing July 24 (T-2 days):** Brazil's 25% forced-labor Section 301 duty activated today. Full 10–12.5% rates on 99.4% of U.S. imports from 60+ countries implement July 24 with no expiry date, no grandfathering, no rate cap. In-transit provision: goods that departed before July 22 and clear customs by July 29 are exempt. This permanently replaces the SCOTUS-struck IEEPA tariffs at slightly higher rates for most importers. ([Jul 22, 2026 — USTR](https://ustr.gov/about/policy-offices/press-office/press-releases/2026/june/ustr-makes-findings-and-proposes-action-60-section-301-investigations-relating-failures-take-action))

- **Canada Section 338 tariffs (effective ~August 16–19):** Trump's 50% tariffs on Canadian autos, dairy, alcohol, wood products, paper, machinery, and textiles take effect in ~25 days. Energy, critical minerals, and some automotive parts are exempted — but USMCA-qualifying goods are explicitly NOT exempt per the proclamation, creating a direct treaty conflict. Legal challenges are being prepared. Cross-border freight impact intensifies post-August 16 for softwood lumber, packaged goods, and auto components moving northbound. ([Jul 21, 2026 — International Trade Insights](https://www.internationaltradeinsights.com/2026/07/president-trump-imposes-50-tariffs-on-certain-canadian-products-under-section-338-of-the-tariff-act/))

- **USMCA Round 3 (July 21–23, Mexico City):** Third bilateral US-Mexico negotiating session underway through July 23, covering automotive rules of origin (US demands 82% RVC + 50% US-specific content + Chinese component restrictions), steel and aluminum, agriculture, and electronic payments. Canada remains excluded. With the US simultaneously imposing 50% tariffs on Canada, trilateral resolution before the next annual review clock is unlikely. ([Jul 21, 2026 — USTR](https://ustr.gov/about/policy-offices/press-office/press-releases/2026/july/united-states-and-mexico-convene-mexico-city-third-bilateral-negotiating-round-related-joint-review))

### 💡 CPO Lens: 3 Things Worth Discussing with Your Team

1. **Overroute as a category signal:** J.B. Hunt just commercialized its internal AI freight execution toolkit as a standalone carrier-facing product. Should Uber Freight build the equivalent — an AI execution layer that's carrier-native, embedded in carrier workflows — to deepen carrier relationships and reduce cost-to-serve before carriers adopt third-party tools that reduce their need for brokers? What would "Uber Freight's Overroute" look like on the carrier side of our platform?

2. **Section 301 Q3 positioning:** With tariff compliance paralysis hitting spot bookings now and a record August import surge expected, the brokerage team faces a bipolar demand environment: freeze this week, flood in 3–4 weeks. Is our spot-to-contract capacity positioning set up to capture the August surge, or will commitments made during the freeze leave us short when shippers scramble? How should the managed transportation team be proactively reaching out to shipper clients right now?

3. **USMCA + Canada 50% tariff compounding for cross-border:** Uber Freight's strong US-Mexico cross-border position is a competitive differentiator exactly when Canada cross-border freight is heading into disruption (50% tariffs August 16 + no USMCA renewal). Which enterprise 4PL shippers in the Transplace book have significant Canadian freight that could be rerouted to Mexico or domestic alternatives, and do we have a product motion to help them do that?

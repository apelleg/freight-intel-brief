🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-06-10

Read time: ~4 minutes

⚡ Today's Top Signal

Uber sold 67.5 million Aurora shares at $7.10/share ($479M) on June 2 — cutting its stake from ~19% to 15.6% — and Aurora's stock (AUR) has continued sliding, closing at $6.16 on June 9, now 13% below the block sale price and down 25% since May 14. For Uber Freight, this is more than a financial footnote: Aurora is Uber Freight's primary autonomous trucking carrier partner (one of 7 commercial customers, 370K driverless miles in Q1), yet Uber parent is clearly monetizing its position. The critical implication: if Aurora's stock weakness impairs its ability to raise capital for the Gen-2 hardware ramp and 200-truck year-end target, Uber Freight's AV lane commitments could slip — forcing an immediate product strategy decision about whether to diversify across Waabi, Kodiak, and PlusAI or double down on Aurora.

📊 Market Pulse (30 seconds)

  • Spot rates: Dry van continuing to firm above $2.50/mile with DAT reporting "spot truckload rates continue to strengthen" for the week ending June 9 (June 9, 2026 — The Trucker); flatbed ~$3.09–$3.60/mile driven by ongoing data center construction demand; reefer elevated on summer produce season
  • Load-to-truck: Van 12.92 (vs. April avg 7.2), reefer 26.97, flatbed 87.22 as of week of June 3 — expected to tighten further as Amazon Prime Day demand (June 23–26) builds over the next 13 days
  • Notable capacity event: Sparhawk Trucking (178 trucks, 47-year-old Wisconsin carrier, $35M revenue) filed WARN notices May 28 and is now seeking a buyer through its Chapter 11 case — one of 8+ carrier failures in early June as trucking employment hits its lowest level in more than a decade (June 2026 — Trucking Dive)

🏢 Competitor Moves

  • Amazon / Prime Day freight surge: Amazon Prime Day confirmed for June 23–26 — the earliest slot in the event's 11-year history, 3 weeks ahead of the traditional July timing. With Amazon's 80,000 dry-van trailer fleet pre-positioned and FBA inbound closed, third-party brokers face a 13-day window to capture overflow demand before Amazon's captive network absorbs it. Implication for UF: Real-time capacity visibility and shipper proactive outreach will be directly tested; this is also a live benchmark for platform loyalty ahead of Q3 contract renewals. Confidence: High (June 8, 2026 — RMW Commerce)
  • Uber Freight Q2 market report (June 5): CEO Rebecca Tinucci's debut market brief flagged three converging pressures: diesel at $5.38/gal, counter-seasonal capacity tightening (supply-driven, not demand recovery), and regulatory headwinds (FMCSA enforcement + Section 301 tariff cliff July 24). Contract rates are now up 5–6% YoY excl. fuel; trucking employment is at its lowest level in more than a decade. Implication for UF: The self-published urgency framing positions UF as a proactive shipper advisor, but also confirms Q2 will stress-test its carrier network and pricing models simultaneously. Confidence: High (June 5, 2026 — The Trucker)

Robinson AI-driven consolidation: CHR stock is up 46% over 52 weeks on its 30-AI-agent deployment (100% rate quotes in 32 seconds) and post-SCOTUS broker consolidation thesis; RXO is now below investment-grade (Moody's) and down 50% over the same period. Implication for UF: Enterprise shippers are actively consolidating broker relationships to fewer, better-capitalized platforms — Uber Freight must earn its seat at that table now, before Q3 RFPs close. Confidence: Medium (June 2026 — STATTimes)

🔧 Freight Tech & AI

  • SELF DRIVE Act passes full House Energy & Commerce Committee: H.R. 7390 cleared the full committee this week (following its 12-11 subcommittee vote), now heading to a House floor vote or inclusion in surface transportation reauthorization. Key freight-specific provision: allows AV trucks to haul commercial freight during testing/evaluation, preempts all state-level restrictions, and sets federal NHTSA safety standards due September 2027. This removes the multistate patchwork barrier that has confined Aurora, Waabi, and Kodiak to Texas corridors — accelerating national AV freight deployment timelines by potentially 12–18 months. (June 2026 — Fox News / ACT News)
  • SAP ships agentic supply chain AI into TMS: SAP's Transportation Management platform now includes AI agents that plan, execute, and optimize shipments autonomously — carbon-aware routing to real-time exception resolution — without human input. Gartner forecasts supply chain management AI software growing from $2B in 2025 to $53B by 2030. The critical risk for Uber Freight: as enterprise TMS platforms embed agentic AI natively, the standalone case for an AI-native freight broker layer weakens unless UF demonstrates superior carrier data density and execution outcomes. (June 2026 — SAP News Center)
  • Drewry WCI surges 23% to $3,433/FEU: As of June 4, Shanghai–LA spot hit $4,565/FEU (+31% WoW) and Shanghai–NY reached $5,505/FEU (+20% WoW), with only 3 blank sailings on transpacific next week vs. prior-week averages. GRI filings targeting $7,000/FEU East Coast are on the table. Driver: Section 301 tariff cliff July 24 accelerating import front-loading simultaneously with Amazon Prime Day pulling peak season 3–4 weeks early. (June 4, 2026 — Drewry/gCaptain)

📋 Regulatory Watch

  • USMCA Round 2 opens this week — June 16–17, Washington DC: The second US-Mexico bilateral round covers agriculture and "level playing field" provisions. Trade analysts now broadly expect the July 1 joint review deadline to be missed, shifting to an annual review cycle through 2036. Key freight risk: automotive/industrial rules-of-origin tightening could reshape the nearshoring freight volumes driving Mexico's 15.5% US import share — Uber Freight's highest-growth cross-border corridor. (June 2026 — USTR / Mexico Business News)
  • Section 301 tariff July 7 hearing — 6-week countdown: USTR's public hearing is July 7; implementation expected as soon as July 24. Proposed rates: 10% on Mexico, Canada, EU, Ecuador, Indonesia, Pakistan; 12.5% on 54 other economies. Any implementation at or above current IEEPA levels triggers another import front-loading wave into an already-tight spot market. (June 2026 — Dorsey / USTR)

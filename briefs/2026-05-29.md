## 🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-05-29
**Read time: ~4 minutes**

### ⚡ Today's Top Signal
The first U.S.-Mexico bilateral USMCA negotiating round concluded today in Mexico City (May 28-29), with Deputy USTR Jeff Goettman and Mexico's Economy Minister Marcelo Ebrard leading talks specifically on rules of origin for vehicles and key industrial goods. Three rounds are now scheduled before the July 1 joint review deadline, with a bipartisan House Ways and Means delegation and 60 business representatives participating. For Uber Freight, this is the most material regulatory development in months: the cross-border Mexico corridor now represents 15.5% of U.S. imports and is UF's fastest-growing freight vertical — and tightened rules of origin on automotive and industrial goods could reduce the near-shored manufacturing volume that drives cross-border demand before Q4. (May 29, 2026 — [Mexico Business News](https://mexicobusiness.news/trade-and-investment/news/first-usmca-joint-review-round-opens-mexico-city))

### 📋 Regulatory Watch
**USMCA First Bilateral Round — Mexico City (May 28-29, 2026):** The U.S. and Mexico concluded the first of three bilateral negotiating rounds today in Mexico City, with rules of origin for vehicles and key industrial goods as the primary agenda item. A bipartisan House Ways and Means delegation and 60 business representatives accompanied USTR Goettman. **Second round:** June 16-17 in Washington D.C., covering agriculture and level-playing-field provisions. **July 1:** Full USMCA joint review kickoff with all three parties. Risk profile: Tightened domestic content rules for automotive/industrial goods assembled in Mexico would reduce near-shored manufacturing volumes — and thus the cross-border truckload demand that underpins Uber Freight's fastest-growing corridor. (May 28-29, 2026 — [USTR](https://ustr.gov/about/policy-offices/press-office/press-releases/2026/may/united-states-and-mexico-announce-series-bilateral-negotiating-rounds-related-first-joint-review) / [Trucks, Parts & Service](https://www.truckpartsandservice.com/regulations/industry/article/15826315/rules-of-origin-take-center-stage-in-bilateral-trade-talks))

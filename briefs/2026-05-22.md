## 🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-05-22
**Read time: ~4 minutes**

### ⚡ Today's Top Signal

The *Montgomery* SCOTUS broker liability ruling — two weeks old — is now producing its first concrete structural effects on freight market competition. Two fresh FreightWaves analyses published this week conclude that broker contingent auto liability insurance premiums could increase 3–5x (some renewal quotes are coming in at +300%), and the compounded compliance burden creates an existential threat for smaller brokers while handing a structural tailwind to scaled 3PLs. C.H. Robinson and RXO both stated publicly this week that the ruling "will accelerate industry consolidation." For Uber Freight — which has existing carrier safety infrastructure, fraud monitoring, and onboarding controls — this is the clearest near-term competitive moat opportunity in years, but capturing it requires the product team to move now on building a carrier safety surface that is visible and marketable to enterprise shippers.

LMI Transportation Prices hit 95.0 — near the highest reading ever recorded, driven by Strait of Hormuz fuel disruption and shrinking capacity pools. - **Notable capacity event:** Memorial Day oversize/overweight restrictions begin **today** (May 22) through May 26 in most states — NY, MO, WA and others halt all OS/OW flatbed moves starting noon today. Flatbed shippers who didn't pre-stage face a 4-day freeze on a segment already 27% above prior-year rates. ### 🔧 Freight Tech & AI

- **Post-Montgomery: broker insurance gap confirmed as real**: FreightWaves published "The freight broker insurance gap is now real" — contingent auto liability and professional liability premiums are expected to increase 3–5x for brokers, with some renewal discussions opening at +300%. At 1–3% of revenue as baseline, a 5x increase turns insurance into an existential cost line for thin-margin brokers — and makes carrier safety vetting infrastructure a legal necessity, not a product feature. (<a href="https://www.freightwaves.com/news/the-freight-broker-insurance-gap-is-now-real">May 2026 — FreightWaves</a>, <a href="https://beinsure.com/news/freight-brokers-face-new-insurance-gap-after-court-ruling/">BeInsure</a>)

- **Maverick Transportation: flatbed carrier wage inflation accelerating**: Maverick announces +4 cents/mile for all flatbed OTR drivers effective May 31, pushing starting pay to 59 cents/mile (~$73K–$80K/year first-year) and experienced driver pay to 63–67 cents/mile (~$84K–$96K/year). More than 10 dedicated flatbed and glass divisions also receiving increases. Signal: carrier operating cost inflation is now rising faster than spot rate normalization — the squeeze on broker margins will intensify into Q3. (<a href="https://www.ttnews.com/articles/maverick-raises-pay-flatbed">May 2026 — Transport Topics</a>)

- **Pallet raises $27M for workflow AI**: AI workers for freight brokers, 3PLs, carriers, and shippers — automating scheduling, document collection, and shipment coordination. Part of a growing infrastructure layer of agentic tools competing for workflow automation just below the TMS. As insurance costs force smaller brokers to cut headcount, these tools accelerate the hollowing out of mid-market freight labor. (<a href="https://www.dcvelocity.com/technology/artificial-intelligence/tech-startup-pallet-raises-27-million-for-workflow-ai">2026 — DC Velocity</a>)

### 📋 Regulatory Watch

**Memorial Day OS/OW restrictions active starting today (May 22)**: Oversize and overweight freight moves are restricted in most states beginning at noon today through May 26. New York halts OS/OW moves from noon May 22 to sunrise May 26; Missouri, Washington, and most other states follow similar restrictions. This is a material operational constraint on an equipment category already up 27% YoY in spot rates — shippers with OS/OW flatbed moves that weren't staged by this morning face a multi-day freeze. ### 💡 CPO Lens: 3 Things Worth Discussing with Your Team

1. **Carrier Safety as a Marketable Product**: Post-*Montgomery*, the carrier vetting layer is now a legal liability management function with direct financial consequences for enterprise shippers. Should Uber Freight productize its existing carrier safety infrastructure — real-time CSA scoring, onboarding controls, fraud monitoring — as an explicit, branded differentiator that justifies a contract premium over smaller brokers now facing 3–5x insurance cost increases? This is an opportunity to turn a compliance function into a revenue lever.

2. **60-Day Consolidation Window**: FreightWaves and industry observers describe smaller brokers as facing an "existential threat" from the *Montgomery* insurance cost cliff. The 60–90 day window before insurance renewal cycles hit is the moment when distressed mid-market brokerages will be most open to selling shipper books, carrier relationships, or talent. Is Uber Freight actively monitoring this market for acquisition or recruitment opportunities — and does the commercial team have a playbook ready?

3. **Flatbed as a Strategic Expansion Lane**: Flatbed spot rates are up 27% YoY, Memorial Day OS/OW restrictions are halting moves starting today, and data center construction is sustaining $41B/year in flatbed-intensive cargo demand concentrated in the same Sun Belt corridors where Uber Freight already operates. Given UF's current dry van concentration, is there a case for accelerating flatbed network investment or a carrier partnership play to capture construction and industrial freight demand at current margins before that window tightens further?

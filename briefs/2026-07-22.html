🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-07-22

Read time: ~4 minutes

⚡ Today's Top Signal

Section 301 tariffs have reached their final countdown: Brazil's 25% forced-labor duty activated this morning (July 22), and the broader 10–12.5% on 60+ countries implements in 48 hours (July 24). This is the most consequential tariff transition of the year — replacing the SCOTUS-voided IEEPA rates with no grandfathering, no rate cap, and no expiry date. The immediate freight signal is a compliance-induced demand pause: load posts fell 9% WoW this week as shippers froze bookings awaiting clarity. For Uber Freight, the strategic question is two-sided: the brokerage must navigate a Q3 demand trough as shipper planning halts, while the 4PL/managed transportation team has a concrete window to demonstrate AI-driven network replanning value before the August import surge — which NRF projects will hit a record 2.47M TEU — hits domestic truckload networks.

📋 Regulatory Watch

  • Section 301 tariffs implementing July 24 (T-2 days): Brazil's 25% forced-labor Section 301 duty activated today. Full 10–12.5% rates on 99.4% of U.S. imports from 60+ countries implement July 24 with no expiry date, no grandfathering, no rate cap. In-transit provision: goods that departed before July 22 and clear customs by July 29 are exempt. Permanently replaces the SCOTUS-struck IEEPA tariffs at slightly higher rates for most importers. (Jul 22, 2026 — USTR)
  • Canada Section 338 tariffs (effective ~August 16–19): Trump's 50% tariffs on Canadian autos, dairy, alcohol, wood products, paper, machinery, and textiles take effect in ~25 days. Energy, critical minerals, and some automotive parts are exempted — but USMCA-qualifying goods are explicitly NOT exempt per the proclamation, creating a direct treaty conflict. Legal challenges are being prepared. Cross-border freight impact intensifies post-August 16 for softwood lumber, packaged goods, and auto components. (Jul 21, 2026 — International Trade Insights)
  • USMCA Round 3 (July 21–23, Mexico City): Third bilateral US-Mexico negotiating session underway through July 23 covering automotive rules of origin (US demands 82% RVC + 50% US-specific content + Chinese component restrictions), steel and aluminum, agriculture, and electronic payments. Canada excluded. Simultaneous 50% tariff on Canada makes trilateral resolution unlikely before the next annual review clock. (Jul 21, 2026 — USTR)

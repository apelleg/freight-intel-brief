## 🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-06-04
**Read time: ~4 minutes**

### ⚡ Today's Top Signal
On June 2–3, 2026, USTR proposed sweeping new Section 301 forced-labor tariffs of 10–12.5% on imports from 60 economies — including Mexico, Canada, the EU, and 54 additional countries — with a public hearing set for July 7 and potential implementation in Q3 2026. For Uber Freight, this is the single biggest near-term freight catalyst: shippers with Mexico cross-border exposure (Uber Freight's stated strategic corridor) face a new front-loading window that will compress into the 4–5 weeks before any action takes effect, layering directly onto already-elevated capacity tightness. The tariff's forced-labor framing makes it politically durable and unlikely to be reversed quickly — Uber Freight's cross-border and enterprise shipper products need an immediate response plan.

- **Load-to-truck:** Van 12.92, reefer 26.97, flatbed 87.22 vs. April averages of 7.2 / 13.5 / 72.3 — historically tight (June 2026 — DAT)
- **Notable capacity event:** Amazon Prime Day confirmed June 23 — domestic freight demand wave building 3–4 weeks earlier than 2025, with intermodal up 14% YoY and international container rail +8% YoY as shippers shift to rail on spot truckload sticker shock (June 1–5, 2026 — Transportation Insight)

### 🏢 Competitor Moves
- **XPO (LTL):** Reported May 2026 LTL operating data June 3: tonnage +0.5% YoY driven by +3.3% more shipments per day, offset by -2.7% weight per shipment. *Implication for UF: Shippers are splitting loads smaller — a signal of freight-value stress and spillover from elevated truckload rates. Uber Freight's shipper base likely faces the same mix shift, which could weigh on gross bookings per load.* Confidence: High (June 3, 2026 — GlobeNewswire/XPO)

- **FedEx Freight (FDXF):** Day 4 of independent trading: Raymond James initiated Outperform $180 PT and Wolfe Research initiated Outperform $180 PT, both citing LTL pricing cycle tailwinds and margin improvement upside as a standalone public company; stock at ~$153 with $180 consensus target. *Implication for UF: As FDXF faces investor pressure to improve operating ratio, it will price more aggressively — bullish for multi-mode rate environment UF operates in, but also means a more formidable LTL competitor in shipper relationships.* Confidence: High (June 2–4, 2026 — Investing.com)

- **DHL eCommerce / USPS:** DHL eCommerce signed a $10B+ exclusive long-term final-mile agreement with USPS (May 28), with DHL handling pickup/sortation/linehaul and USPS completing last-mile delivery to 170M delivery points across 41,550 ZIP codes. *Implication for UF: This directly competes with Uber Freight's Better Trucks last-mile investment; DHL+USPS creates a scaled residential final-mile network that could undercut 3PL-managed last-mile economics for small parcel and e-commerce shippers who are the target of UF's last-mile strategy.* Confidence: High (May 28, 2026 — DHL Group)

### 🔧 Freight Tech & AI
- **SELF DRIVE Act advances to full committee:** The bipartisan SELF DRIVE Act (H.R. 7390) passed House Energy & Commerce subcommittee 12–11 and is now heading to full committee markup — the first federal bill that would authorize driverless commercial trucks nationally, preempt all state AV restrictions, and allow self-certification via company-developed safety cases with no mandatory pre-deployment government review. OOIDA formally opposes but Republican majority is pushing through. (June 2026 — iDispatchHub / Congress.gov)

- **Intermodal diverges from truckload:** Domestic intermodal +14% YoY, international container rail +8% YoY in the June 1–5 week, as 30–40% YoY spot truckload rates push shippers to rail. The ~$1.20/mile arbitrage between intermodal ($1.40) and truckload spot ($2.60) is closing but still meaningful — Uber Freight's intermodal product is in a strong demand window right now. (June 1–5, 2026 — Transportation Insight)

- **AI automation now table stakes:** As RXO automates 500K+ carrier calls/quarter and CHR handles 100% of rate quotes in 32 seconds with 30 AI agents, back-office AI is no longer a differentiator in freight brokerage — it is the minimum competitive bar. The differentiation is now shifting to intelligence (market prediction, shipper cost modeling, AV load routing). (June 2026 — FreightWaves State of Industry)

### 📋 Regulatory Watch
**USTR Section 301 Forced Labor Tariffs — 60 Economies (June 2, 2026):** USTR proposed 10% additional tariffs on Canada, Mexico, Ecuador, the EU, Indonesia, and Pakistan (partial prohibitions, poor enforcement) and 12.5% on 54 other economies with no forced labor import prohibition at all. Public hearing July 7, 2026; written comments due July 6. Potential implementation Q3 2026. Mexico's inclusion directly threatens the $2T+ North American trade corridor that is Uber Freight's primary cross-border growth market. (June 2–3, 2026 — USTR / CNBC)

**USMCA July 1 Deadline Unlikely to Be Met:** Multiple trade experts now confirm a clean 16-year USMCA extension by July 1 is unlikely, given bilateral (not trilateral) talks and no agreed text. If extension fails, the agreement enters a cycle of annual reviews through 2036 — persistent uncertainty for cross-border freight contracts, manufacturer supply chains, and shipper planning. (June 2026 — Mexico Business News / CSIS)

### 💡 CPO Lens: 3 Things Worth Discussing with Your Team

1. **Cross-Border Tariff Intelligence:** USTR's Section 301 forced-labor tariff proposal now puts Mexico inside a potential 10% additional duty — on top of IEEPA tariff stacking already in effect — creating a new front-loading window in July before Q3 implementation. Should we build a real-time tariff intelligence feature into Uber Freight's shipper product, so enterprise customers can see their all-in landed cost and freight-mode tradeoffs in one view, rather than managing tariff complexity and freight execution separately?

2. **Last-Mile Positioning Post DHL-USPS:** The $10B+ DHL-USPS exclusive final-mile deal means the US's most comprehensive residential delivery network just partnered with a major international logistics operator. Does Uber Freight's Better Trucks investment still offer sufficient coverage and differentiation against this scaled incumbent, or do we need to deepen our last-mile stack — or pivot our value proposition toward commercial/B2B middle-mile where we have a more defensible advantage?

3. **AV-Native Load Routing:** The SELF DRIVE Act moving through committee means federal AV authorization for commercial trucks could land within 12–18 months. Aurora, Waabi, and Kodiak already run commercial loads on Uber Freight's network. When federal authorization removes remaining deployment constraints, AV load volumes could scale rapidly. Are our load-matching, pricing, and settlement systems ready to route and settle driverless loads at scale — or will we need to build AV-native capabilities before that wave hits?

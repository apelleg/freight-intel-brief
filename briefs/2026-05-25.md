## 🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-05-25
**Read time: ~4 minutes**

### ⚡ Today's Top Signal
C.H. Robinson is going on offense in Uber Freight's most strategically important corridor. On May 23, Robinson Fresh opened a **$33M, 142,600 sq ft cold-chain facility in Pharr, Texas** — just 4.5 miles from the Pharr-Reynosa International Bridge, the single most critical US–Mexico crossing for perishables (98% of Mexican fresh produce enters the US through TX, NM, AZ, or CA; Texas alone handles 55%). This is not a marginal facility expansion — it is CHR building hard physical infrastructure at the exact border point FreightWaves called "US trucking's biggest stabilizer in 2026." With 69 dock doors, multi-temperature zones, GFSI certification, and a footprint large enough to consolidate and replace two prior Texas facilities, CHR is extending its reefer moat into the corridor where Uber Freight is betting its cross-border growth.

### 🔧 Freight Tech & AI
- **AI tools are multiplying decisions, not eliminating them.** A new Deep Current survey (DC Velocity, May 2026) of freight and logistics leaders found 74% make 50+ operational decisions per day, 50% make 100+, and 83% operate in "reactive mode" — *despite* widespread AI tool adoption. Root cause: 68% use 5 or more systems daily with fragmented data across them. This is both a validation of Uber Freight's unified intelligence platform thesis and a warning: adding another AI tool without deep integration doesn't solve the problem. ([DC Velocity](https://www.dcvelocity.com/technology/artificial-intelligence/survey-despite-ai-tools-freight-professionals-make-even-more-decisions-per-day), May 2026)

- **Waabi + Volvo VNL Autonomous in production.** Unveiled at ACT Expo on May 5, the Volvo VNL Autonomous truck powered by Waabi Driver runs NVIDIA DRIVE AGX Thor/Hyperion 10 architecture with six redundant safety systems — and is already manufactured at Volvo's New River Valley VA plant. Waabi's end-to-end AI handles both highway and surface streets, targeting driver-out on Texas corridors with Uber Freight loads by year-end 2026. *(May 5, 2026 — [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/10/28/waabi-unveils-autonomous-truck-made-in-partnership-with-volvo/), [FleetOwner](https://www.fleetowner.com/technology/media-gallery/55376411/volvo-autonomous-semitruck-a-close-look-at-waabis-self-driving-vnl-tractor/))*

- **Uber Autonomous Solutions commercialization layer expands.** Uber's Feb 2026 launch of Uber Autonomous Solutions (infrastructure, UX, fleet ops as a service for AV partners) is gaining traction — VW partnership for LA robotaxi late 2026, and Aurora/Waabi both use Uber as a freight commercialization channel. This positions Uber's network as a load-supply backbone for multiple competing AV stacks simultaneously. *(Feb 23, 2026 — [Uber IR](https://investor.uber.com/news-events/news/press-release-details/2026/Uber-Unveils-Uber-Autonomous-Solutions-to-Accelerate-Autonomous-Mobility--Delivery-Worldwide/default.aspx))*

### 📋 Regulatory Watch
**Montgomery ruling formally extends to shippers.** New legal analysis from IndexBox and Benesch Law (May 2026) confirms the Supreme Court's 9-0 reasoning in Montgomery v. Caribe Transport II applies not just to freight brokers but to **shippers** — enterprise shippers who select brokers or carriers without documented vetting procedures now face analogous negligent hiring exposure under state law. Insurance underwriters are requiring documented carrier-selection policies as a coverage condition. This creates a material compliance driver for enterprise shippers to demand more rigorous carrier safety verification from digital freight platforms, including Uber Freight. *(May 2026 — [IndexBox](https://www.indexbox.io/blog/supreme-court-ruling-may-expose-shippers-to-liability-warn-industry-leaders/), [Benesch Law](https://www.beneschlaw.com/insight/one-battle-after-another-freight-brokers-in-a-post-montgomery-world/))*

### 💡 CPO Lens: 3 Things Worth Discussing with Your Team

1. **Cross-border produce cold chain:** CHR just put $33M of physical infrastructure at Pharr TX — the most important US–Mexico produce crossing. Can Uber Freight build a "tech-first Pharr" — deep carrier aggregation, real-time cold-chain visibility, and automated compliance handoffs — that out-competes physical infrastructure with service reliability? What's the current reefer carrier depth and on-time performance on this specific corridor?

2. **AI tool fragmentation paradox:** The Deep Current survey shows freight professionals using 5+ disconnected systems are making *more* decisions per day despite AI adoption, not fewer. Is Uber Freight's platform reducing system count for shippers, or is it adding a 6th system? What's the net impact on customer decision load — and how do we measure it?

3. **FDXF independence begins in 7 days:** FedEx Freight becomes an independent public company June 1 with its own investor pressure to grow revenue 4–6% and reach 12% OR. As a standalone carrier, FDXF will reprice LTL selectively and aggressively in lanes where it has density advantages. Which Uber Freight shipper accounts have high LTL exposure in FDXF-dominant lanes, and are we proactively positioning alternatives now?

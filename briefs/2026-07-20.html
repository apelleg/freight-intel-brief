🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-07-20

Read time: ~4 minutes

⚡ Today's Top Signal

U.S. container imports are on track to hit an all-time record 2.47M TEU in July — eclipsing the May 2022 pandemic peak — as shippers front-load ahead of Section 301 tariffs implementing in four days (July 24). The reversal is already locked in: NRF projects August imports will drop to 2.22M TEU (-4.5% YoY), a roughly 10% month-over-month collapse in inbound volume. For Uber Freight, this is the demand cliff that matters most: the brokerage side faces a sudden spot-demand evaporation while 4PL/managed transportation clients will need TMS-driven mode optimization and carrier flexibility tools to absorb the whiplash. The record-import surge is also what has kept spot rates 40-50% above year-ago levels — when it ends, the rate narrative shifts overnight.

📋 Regulatory Watch

  • Section 301 tariffs: T-4 days (effective July 24, 2026): New duties of 10-12.5% on 99.4% of U.S. imports by value replace the expiring Section 122 10% global tariff (statutory sunset July 24). Some Section 301 duties already effective July 22 — Brazil at 25%. Duty rate set by entry date; no extension legislation pending. NRF projects July imports will hit an all-time record 2.47M TEU from front-loading, then fall to 2.22M TEU in August (-4.5% YoY). (June 2026 — USTR; July 2026 — NRF)
  • FMCSA three deregulatory rules effective July 22 (2 days): Removes CDL self-reporting requirement, rescinds the physical in-cab ELD manual, revises roadside inspection corrected-report procedures. (July 2026 — McFarlane Law)
  • USMCA Round 3 begins July 21 (tomorrow): US-Mexico bilateral talks in Mexico City. Key US demands: automotive rules of origin raised to 82% regional content (from 75%), plus new 50% US-specific content requirement, plus restrictions on Chinese components. Open issues reduced from 54 to 14. Canada excluded. U.S. chose annual review over 16-year renewal at July 1 deadline — cross-border uncertainty locked in through at least 2027. (July 2026 — USTR; Rio Times Online)

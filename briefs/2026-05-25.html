🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-05-25

⚡ Today's Top Signal

C.H. Robinson is going on offense in Uber Freight's most strategically important corridor. On May 23, Robinson Fresh opened a $33M, 142,600 sq ft cold-chain facility in Pharr, Texas — just 4.5 miles from the Pharr-Reynosa International Bridge, the single most critical US–Mexico crossing for perishables (98% of Mexican fresh produce enters the US through TX, NM, AZ, or CA; Texas alone handles 55%). This is not a marginal facility expansion — it is CHR building hard physical infrastructure at the exact border point FreightWaves called "US trucking's biggest stabilizer in 2026." With 69 dock doors, multi-temperature zones, GFSI certification, and a footprint large enough to consolidate and replace two prior Texas facilities, CHR is extending its reefer moat into the corridor where Uber Freight is betting its cross-border growth.

🔧 Freight Tech & AI

  • AI tools are multiplying decisions, not eliminating them. A new Deep Current survey (DC Velocity, May 2026) of freight and logistics leaders found 74% make 50+ operational decisions per day, 50% make 100+, and 83% operate in "reactive mode" — despite widespread AI tool adoption. Root cause: 68% use 5 or more systems daily with fragmented data. This is both a validation of Uber Freight's unified intelligence platform thesis and a warning: adding another AI tool without deep integration doesn't solve the problem.
  • Waabi + Volvo VNL Autonomous in production. Waabi's end-to-end AI handles both highway and surface streets, targeting driver-out on Texas corridors with Uber Freight loads by year-end 2026. (TechCrunch, FleetOwner, May 2026)

Uber's Feb 2026 launch of Uber Autonomous Solutions (infrastructure, UX, fleet ops as a service for AV partners) is gaining traction — VW partnership for LA robotaxi late 2026, with Aurora and Waabi both using Uber as a freight commercialization channel. This positions Uber's network as a load-supply backbone for multiple competing AV stacks simultaneously. (Feb 23, 2026 — Uber IR)

🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-05-19

Read time: ~4 minutes

⚡ Today's Top Signal

CNBC published its 2026 Disruptor 50 list today, ranking Waabi at #27 — with the disclosure that Uber has committed $250M to deploy at least 25,000 Waabi autonomous vehicles, while Waabi's self-driving big rigs are already running commercial freight routes in Texas for Uber Freight, Samsung, and Fortune 500 shippers. The implication is layered: Uber's corporate parent is simultaneously a strategic funder, a commercial customer, and now a legitimizer of an AV platform that Uber Freight operationally depends on — and former Uber Freight founder Lior Ron now serves as COO at Waabi. With Waabi targeting fully driverless operation across the U.S. Southwest by year-end 2026, the incoming CPO joins at exactly the moment this partnership either becomes Uber Freight's most powerful capability differentiator or its most complex strategic dependency.

📊 Market Pulse (30 seconds)

  • Spot rates: Dry van ~$2.80/mile all-in nationally (+23% YoY per SONAR NTI.USA); post-Roadcheck week normalization underway but rates remain at multi-year highs. Flatbed $3.05+/mile, fueled by the AI data center construction surge. (May 19, 2026 — SONAR / DAT)
  • Load-to-truck: Reefer 16.4 vs. 14.5 prior week; OTRI 13.4% nationally with Midwest above 18% — carrier leverage at highest since early 2022. (May 18, 2026 — FreightWaves SONAR)
  • Notable capacity event: LTL networks now absorbing truckload overflow — new LTL bids pricing 12.5% higher year-over-year, strongest upward pressure since the 2023 national carrier exit. Shippers are hitting rate ceilings across TL, LTL, and intermodal simultaneously.

🏢 Competitor Moves

  • Loop (AI-native supply chain): Launched its Logistics Data Platform (LDP) on May 4 with a $95M Series C led by Valor Equity Partners (Founders Fund, 8VC, Index Ventures, J.P. Morgan Growth Equity among backers). The platform positions as an "autonomous system that executes complex logistics strategies" — combining a validated data engine (DUX 2.0), autonomous exception agents, and an AI Assistant targeting enterprise shippers like Dot Foods, Clemens Food Group, and Kendra Scott. Implication for UF: Loop is aiming directly at the same enterprise shipper decision-maker with a data-first product strategy that could commoditize freight visibility and exception management; the race to own the shipper intelligence layer is accelerating in real time. Confidence: High (May 4, 2026 — BusinessWire)
  • RXO: Q1 2026 results (May 7) triggered a wave of analyst upgrades — Goldman Sachs raised price target to $18 (from $13), Truist to $26 (from $20), UBS to $22 (from $17). CEO Drew Wilkerson called RXO "well positioned for the upturn." Analysts are uniformly bullish on scale asset-light brokers in the current tightening cycle. Implication for UF: RXO is receiving public market credit for its scale + tech combination during the sector's best rate recovery in years; Uber Freight must articulate its own margin expansion narrative or risk being undervalued in the same favorable environment. Confidence: High (May 7, 2026 — RXO/Analyst Reports)
  • LTL incumbents (Old Dominion, ABF, Saia, XPO LTL): ODFL announced a 4.9% general rate increase; new LTL bid pricing running 12.5% YoY above prior year and 29% above May 2021. Average LTL shipment weight up ~11% YTD as shippers split truckloads seeking capacity. Shippers are turning to LTL as a "release valve" for truckload tightness — but finding limited relief. Implication for UF: Multi-modal shipper flexibility becomes a genuine product moat; the Better Trucks investment and middle-mile expansion position UF uniquely if the product integrates TL + LTL + last-mile in a single workflow. Confidence: High (May 2026 — FreightWaves SONAR/IndexBox)
  • FedEx Freight (FDXF): When-issued trading begins May 29 under "FDXF WI" with NYSE debut June 1 as an independent $9B LTL carrier. Record date was May 15. Implication for UF: A newly independent, publicly accountable FDXF focused exclusively on LTL pricing power and market share will intensify competitive pressure on every TL/LTL hybrid load — and raises the urgency for UF's multi-modal product integration. Confidence: High (May 12, 2026 — BusinessWire)

🔧 Freight Tech & AI

  • Waabi — CNBC Disruptor 50 #27 (May 19, 2026 — CNBC): Self-driving big rigs already running commercially in Texas for Uber Freight and Fortune 500 clients; targeting driver-out U.S. Southwest by end of 2026, full North America within 5 years. Uber's $250M deployment commitment is contingent on scale but the directional commitment is now public. Notably, 43 of 50 companies on the 2026 Disruptor 50 cite GenAI as business-critical — signaling AI-native logistics is the new baseline competitive expectation.
  • Loop Logistics Data Platform (May 4, 2026 — BusinessWire): AI-native supply chain OS for enterprise shippers backed by Founders Fund and J.P. Morgan Growth Equity Partners. CEO Matt McKinney: "We fundamentally shift from a platform that helps humans work faster to an autonomous system that executes complex logistics strategies on their behalf." Joins project44's Autopilot (launched May 11) in a new cohort of agentic freight AI platforms targeting the same enterprise shipper decision-maker as Uber Freight.
  • Data center construction → flatbed demand surge (May 2026 — DAT / Trucking Dive): Data center construction running at $41B/year, up 200%+ since ChatGPT's launch; flatbed spot reached $3.09/mile in March (+37 cents from February). Demand concentrated in Sun Belt states and remote sites — the same geographies where Aurora, Waabi, and Kodiak are scaling AV freight routes. The AI infrastructure buildout is simultaneously generating the specialized freight that autonomous trucks are uniquely suited to handle.

💡 CPO Lens: 3 Things Worth Discussing with Your Team

  1. Waabi: strategic partner, competitive dependency, or both? Uber's $250M Waabi commitment and Lior Ron's COO role there make this far more than a vendor relationship — it's a corporate entanglement. What is Uber Freight's posture on AV carrier exclusivity vs. multi-platform procurement (Aurora, Kodiak, Volvo AV), and should the product team be building autonomous dispatch as a proprietary core competency or treating it as a commodity integration layer?
  2. The "shipper intelligence layer" race just got real: Loop (May 4) and project44 (May 11) both launched agentic AI platforms targeting enterprise shippers in the last two weeks — entering Uber Freight's competitive space from the data side rather than the capacity side. Uber Freight has carrier data, shipment network, and market signals that neither startup has at scale. Is there a product strategy to surface this as a differentiated intelligence product before these platforms entrench themselves with your best shipper accounts?
  3. Multi-modal is a genuine cycle opportunity, not a feature request: With LTL rates 12.5% YoY, truckload spot 23% YoY, and intermodal finally repricing — shippers face simultaneous rate pressure across all modes with no easy substitution. Uber Freight's Better Trucks investment, middle-mile solution, and TL brokerage form the right asset base. Does the product team have a roadmap to surface cross-mode optimization in a single shipper workflow before the summer capacity peak?

## 🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-07-15
**Read time: ~4 minutes**

### ⚡ Today's Top Signal
Trump proposed a 20% toll on all cargo transiting the Strait of Hormuz on Monday (July 13) — then scrapped it Tuesday (July 14) under Gulf ally pressure, replacing it with pledges of direct Gulf state investment in the US. The 24-hour reversal crystallizes the operating environment: Hormuz geopolitics are producing policy whiplash at weekly cadence, the US Iran blockade is back on, and oil is up 10% from last week's brief respite. For freight, this isn't just an energy cost story — it's an import surge story. June container volumes hit 2.4M TEU (+8.2% YoY) with China imports up 27.4% as shippers front-loaded ahead of the Section 301 tariff deadline now nine days away (July 24). That cargo is clearing ports now and will translate into a domestic truck demand surge in the July 28–August 15 window, compounding a truckload market already running at its tightest since February 2022.

### 📋 Regulatory Watch
- **Section 301 tariffs: T-9 days (July 24 implementation):** USTR completed its July 7 public hearing on 10–12.5% tariffs covering 60 economies (99.4% of US imports), replacing the Section 122 10% tariff with no expiry date. June container imports (+8.2% YoY, China +27.4%) prove shippers are front-loading aggressively now. The July 24 cliff — with no built-in extension mechanism — will either trigger an immediate import demand trough or a chaotic transition period; either scenario reshapes spot market dynamics in August. ([July 7, 2026 — USTR](https://ustr.gov/about/policy-offices/press-office/press-releases/2026/june/ustr-makes-findings-and-proposes-action-60-section-301-investigations-relating-failures-take-action) / [Movargo](https://www.movargo.com/post/post-after-july-24-section-301-tariff-2026))

Third bilateral US-Mexico round (July 20, Washington DC) will focus on rules of origin in autos and energy, and China disciplines; Canada remains excluded. Cross-border freight uncertainty persists into 2027. ([July 1, 2026 — USTR/Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/business/2026/07/01/trump-ignores-deadline-extending-usmca-seeks-improved-deal/))

### 💡 CPO Lens: 3 Things Worth Discussing with Your Team

1. **The import surge hits domestic TL in two weeks:** June containers (+8.2% YoY, China +27.4%) are clearing ports now and reaching DCs ahead of the July 24 Section 301 cliff. That freight rolls into domestic spot demand around July 28–August 15 — into a market at $3.05/mile with a flatbed LTR of 53.3. Are we positioned with committed carrier capacity in the key inland distribution corridors (Inland Empire, Chicago, Dallas, Atlanta), and is our pricing engine ready to capture margin in that window before it passes?

2. **Hormuz as a real-time 4PL moment:** Trump's 20% cargo fee was proposed and scrapped in 24 hours — that's the pace of policy change shippers are navigating. With transpacific ocean at $7,500–9,000/FEU, some lanes and SKUs now favor domestic repositioning, air freight, or intermodal alternatives over waiting for ocean capacity. Are managed-transportation customers on the Transplace platform getting proactive mode-shift recommendations when ocean economics shift this dramatically — or are we only reactive when they call?

3. **TMS platform defense as ERP incumbents go agentic:** Blue Yonder (19th consecutive Gartner TMS MQ Leader) and SAP (Joule agents inside existing ERP stacks) are both embedding autonomous TMS execution natively in systems large shippers already pay for. What specific Transplace capabilities justify enterprise renewal against "good enough" agents built into the ERP — multimodal cross-carrier optimization, 4PL control tower visibility, carrier relationship depth? Is there a partnership play with SAP or Snowflake (Blue Yonder's substrate) that strengthens our position rather than defending against it?

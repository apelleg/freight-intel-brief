## 🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-07-15
**Read time: ~4 minutes**

### ⚡ Today's Top Signal
US-Iran fighting resumed July 10, effectively closing the Strait of Hormuz to commercial traffic (down to ~8.5M barrels/day from the pre-crisis norm of ~100 ships/day) and reversing a brief diesel cost reprieve — oil jumped 10% and diesel is heading back toward cycle highs nationally. This energy shock lands at the precise moment truckload spot rates crossed above contract for the first time since February 2022 (DAT, July 9), with permanent Section 301 tariffs set to hit 99.4% of U.S. imports in nine days (July 24). For Uber Freight, the combination squeezes broker margins from both directions — rising carrier fuel costs and enterprise shipper contracts priced well below spot — exactly as a new import front-loading wave kicks off.

- **Federal AV framework advancing to House floor:** The BUILD America 250 Act (passed House Transportation Committee 62-2) bundles SELF DRIVE Act provisions into the surface transportation reauthorization bill, creating the first unified 50-state federal framework for driverless commercial trucks, preempting all state restrictions, and setting NHTSA standards due September 2027. (June-July 2026 — Holland & Knight / ACT News)

- **Aurora Innovation:** An independent three-month Edge Case safety audit validated Aurora's Safety Case in July; Craig-Hallum initiated coverage with a Buy/$18 price target, calling Aurora Driver "physical AI" with $100B+ long-term valuation potential; stock up 9% in early July. Gen-2 hardware — enabling fully observerless driverless operations — is launching this quarter on International LT trucks, targeting 200+ commercial trucks by year-end including loads for Uber Freight. (July 2026 — Aurora Innovation IR)

- **Carrier exit wave deepens:** Nine transportation- and logistics-related companies filed for bankruptcy in early July, with nearly 250 additional jobs eliminated across NJ, NC, IL, and CA logistics facilities (July 14, 2026 — FreightWaves). The structural carrier exit into Q3 continues to outpace new-authority formation, tightening supply even as front-loading demand builds.

### 📋 Regulatory Watch
- **FMCSA: Three deregulatory rules effective July 22:** FMCSA finalized three compliance-reduction rules: removes CDL self-reporting requirement for state motor vehicle convictions (now handled via electronic inter-state data exchange); rescinds the physical ELD operator's manual requirement in cabs; revises roadside inspection corrected-report return procedures (only required when the issuing state requests it). Minor administrative relief for carriers; signals continued Duffy-era deregulatory direction. (June 22, 2026 — FMCSA)

- **Section 301 tariffs: 9 days to permanent implementation:** USTR completed its July 7 public hearing on Section 301 forced-labor tariffs covering 60 countries (99.4% of US imports) at 10-12.5% rates; USTR is now reviewing the hearing record before finalizing. These tariffs replace IEEPA — struck down by SCOTUS in February 2026 — and carry no expiry date and no rate cap, having survived 4,000+ legal challenges. Shippers are front-loading aggressively this week before the permanent new tariff structure takes effect around July 24. (July 2026 — USTR)

- **USMCA: Non-renewal confirmed; Round 3 scheduled week of July 20:** The July 1 joint review deadline passed without US agreement to renew USMCA. The agreement remains in force but under annual review. The third bilateral US-Mexico negotiating round is scheduled for the week of July 20, focused on rules of origin (autos, energy) and China-related disciplines; Canada remains excluded. Ambassador Greer has signaled a "significantly improved deal" is required, creating cross-border freight uncertainty that persists into 2027. (July 1, 2026 — USTR / Washington Post)

### 💡 CPO Lens: 3 Things Worth Discussing with Your Team

1. **Section 301 Tariff Cliff:** Permanent 10-12.5% tariffs land on 99.4% of US imports in nine days with no expiry or safety valve. Should the brokerage team proactively offer capacity commitments to enterprise accounts with routing guides already broken in a 60%-above-prior-year spot market, and can Transplace's 4PL platform automate modal switching and HTS re-routing at the speed this permanent tariff reset requires — or does this expose a gap in UF's platform versus AI-native competitors?

2. **CMA CGM as a 4PL Competitor:** The FedEx Supply Chain acquisition gives CMA CGM a 240-location North American managed-transportation footprint with captive ocean-to-warehouse integration — a structural advantage Uber Freight's asset-light model cannot easily replicate. What specific Transplace capabilities justify enterprise shipper retention versus a carrier-owned 4PL bundling ocean, warehousing, and truckload at one price, and is there a top-tier ocean carrier partnership worth exploring to close this gap?

3. **AV Commercial Commitment Window:** Aurora Gen-2 goes observerless this quarter with Uber Freight as a named customer, and the federal AV framework is heading to a House floor vote. The window to lock in preferential AV capacity agreements at structural cost-of-haul rates (well below $3.00+/mile broker market) exists right now, before CHR or Flexport negotiate the same terms. Does UF have a written commercial commitment securing AV capacity pricing with Aurora and Waabi through year-end 2026?

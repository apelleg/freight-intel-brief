## 🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-05-27
**Read time: ~4 minutes**

### ⚡ Today's Top Signal
A US-Iran peace deal to reopen the Strait of Hormuz is "largely negotiated," per President Trump on May 23, with announcement expected imminently. Under the framework, Iran would clear Hormuz mines and reopen the strait toll-free for 60 days while Washington lifts its port blockade and eases oil sanctions. With Brent crude at ~$99/bbl and diesel at $5.38/gal — the primary driver of trucking's 23% YoY rate spike since the February 28 Hormuz closure — a deal could shave $1–2/gal off diesel within weeks, unwinding the fuel-driven component of spot rate inflation almost overnight and reshaping the structural tightening that has powered the current freight bull cycle.

### 🔧 Freight Tech & AI
- **SAFER Transport Act advances in Congress:** HR 8267 (Rep. Brad Knott, April 20, 2026) with Senate companion from Sen. Todd Young (February 2026) would phase out all MC numbers within 5 years, create criminal penalties for carrier registration fraud, and establish an interagency Freight Fraud & Theft Advisory Committee. Bipartisan support but faces a gridlocked House until after November midterms. *Direct product signal: This legislation validates carrier identity verification as a future compliance requirement — Uber Freight's fraud-prevention infrastructure already addresses this; building ahead of the mandate converts a compliance cost into a competitive moat.* ([April–May 2026 — Overdrive/Congress.gov](https://www.overdriveonline.com/regulations/article/15818491/safer-transport-act-would-require-prosecution-of-fraudsters-more))

- **Volvo CARB $197M settlement raises AV/EV fleet economics bar:** CARB settled with Volvo Group North America for $197M over excess diesel emissions from 10,000+ engines (2010–2016 model years); $108M directed to California clean-fleet projects; Volvo books the full charge in Q2 2026. Volvo is simultaneously manufacturing Waabi's autonomous VNL truck at its New River Valley VA plant. *Signal: Diesel regulatory-compliance costs in California are rising sharply, improving AV/EV economics by comparison and accelerating commercial AV adoption for West Coast fleet operators.* ([May 18, 2026 — CARB/Transport Topics](https://ww2.arb.ca.gov/news/california-settles-certification-and-emissions-violations-case-volvo-group-north-america-llc))

### 📋 Regulatory Watch
**USMCA Joint Review commences July 1, 2026 — 35 days away.** The US, Mexico, and Canada officially launch the USMCA's first formal Joint Review on July 1. The Trump administration is expected to seek rules-of-origin tightening, labor enforcement, and supply chain security concessions — potentially disrupting the nearshoring investments that currently drive Mexico's 15.5% share of US imports. Motor carriers are already gaming out contingencies for tariff regime changes and potential IMMEX program modifications. This review directly shapes the cross-border freight market Uber Freight has named as its primary 2026–2027 growth vector. ([March 2026 — USTR](https://ustr.gov/about/policy-offices/press-office/press-releases/2026/march/united-states-and-mexico-launch-review-process-usmca); [May 2026 — CSIS](https://www.csis.org/analysis/usmca-review-2026))

### 💡 CPO Lens: 3 Things Worth Discussing with Your Team

1. **Hormuz deal = sudden rate repricing scenario:** If Trump announces the Iran deal this week and Brent falls toward $75/bbl, diesel could drop $1.50–$2/gal within 30 days — unwinding roughly 10–15 cents/mile from current dry van spot rates. How does Uber Freight's carrier pricing infrastructure adapt to a rapid downward fuel shock, and are carrier rate agreements structured to capture margin on the way down as well as the way up?

2. **FDXF IPO window = LTL enterprise play:** FedEx Freight begins public price discovery today, and over the next 4 weeks enterprise shippers will be evaluating their LTL concentration for the first time in decades with a public benchmark. Is this the moment to brief the enterprise sales team on a "multi-carrier LTL + UF brokerage" story to capture shippers who want resilience against concentration risk in a newly public FDXF?

3. **USMCA July 1 = cross-border strategy stress test:** With 35 days until formal review and Trump targeting rules-of-origin changes in Mexico, what's Uber Freight's cross-border resilience playbook? Does the product team have scenario models for the most likely outcomes — rules-of-origin tightening, IMMEX disruption, tariff regime changes — and their freight volume impact on UF's fastest-growing corridor?

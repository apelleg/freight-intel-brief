## 🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-06-09
**Read time: ~4 minutes**

### ⚡ Today's Top Signal

The SELF DRIVE Act of 2026 (H.R. 7390) is advancing to full House Energy & Commerce Committee markup, with FreightWaves calling it "a game changer for heavy trucking." The bill creates the first federal 50-state unified framework for driverless commercial trucks — preempting all state-level restrictions, authorizing cab-less 80,000-lb freight vehicles, and allowing AV manufacturers to self-certify systems for revenue-generating operations without FMCSA pre-market review. For Uber Freight, this is the regulatory inflection that transforms Aurora, Waabi, and Kodiak from perpetual pilot programs into commercial-scale capacity providers: if enacted, it removes the state-by-state patchwork that has been the primary go-to-market bottleneck for every AV partner in Uber Freight's network — directly accelerating the timeline for Aurora's Gen-2 observer-free fleet (launching Q2 2026) toward the 200-truck year-end target.

### 🔧 Freight Tech & AI

- **SELF DRIVE Act advancing to full committee markup:** H.R. 7390 passed House subcommittee 12-11 and heads to full House Energy & Commerce Committee. Would preempt all state AV restrictions, authorize cab-less 80,000-lb driverless trucks for revenue-generating freight, allow manufacturer self-certification, and create a national AV safety data repository. First federal bill to explicitly authorize driverless semi-trucks in commercial freight service. Aurora, Waabi, Kodiak, and all major AV trucking companies are primary beneficiaries. (June 8-9, 2026 — FreightWaves / Congress.gov)

- **Aurora Gen-2 hardware launching Q2 2026 (imminent):** Observer-free driverless operations on International LT trucks with 50% hardware cost reduction, 1,000m lidar range, and 1M-mile operational lifespan. Roush upfitter targeting 1,000-truck/year production capacity. Uber Freight is a named Gen-2 commercial customer; 200-truck deployment target by year-end. (June 2026 — Aurora Innovation / Investing.com)

- **Amazon deploys 1 million robots, expanding to Europe:** Amazon confirmed 1 million warehouse robots now operational in US and is investing to expand automation to European fulfillment centers. As Amazon Freight's fleet reaches 80,000+ dry van trailers and Prime Day June 23-26 accelerates domestic freight flows, warehouse automation gives Amazon a structural cost advantage that compounds its logistics network threat to third-party brokers. (June 2026 — FreightWaves)

## 🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-06-03

### ⚡ Today's Top Signal

FMCSA formally published its first proposed rulemaking (NPRM) for automated driving systems (ADS) inspection and maintenance standards on commercial trucks — simultaneously, Congress introduced the SELF DRIVE Act of 2026 allowing AV manufacturers to self-certify systems without federal pre-market review. In the same week Aurora's Gen-2 hardware launches for true observer-free operations and Waabi's Uber Freight deployment expands, Washington is writing the rulebook that defines what it means to be a "compliant" driverless carrier. For Uber Freight as a broker deploying autonomous capacity, the FMCSA NPRM is not background regulatory noise: it's the coming standard your carrier onboarding, safety vetting, and liability documentation must eventually pass.

### 🔧 Freight Tech & AI

- **FMCSA proposes first-ever ADS safety standards (new NPRM):** FMCSA published a proposed rule establishing inspection and maintenance standards for ADS-equipped commercial trucks (Level 4+), covering software system checks, sensor calibration requirements, and human fallback protocols. This is the first federal compliance framework governing Aurora, Waabi, and Kodiak deployments. Comment period now open. (June 2026 — [FreightWaves](https://www.freightwaves.com/news/fmcsa-proposes-new-requirements-for-driverless-trucks), [FMCSA](https://www.fmcsa.dot.gov/newsroom/safe-integration-automated-driving-systems-equipped-commercial-motor-vehicles))

- **SELF DRIVE Act of 2026 introduced in Congress:** Legislation introduced allowing AV manufacturers to self-certify safety compliance without NHTSA/FMCSA pre-market review. If passed, removes the largest timing obstacle to factory-built AV truck deployment at scale in 2027; Aurora/Volvo, Waabi/Volvo, and PlusAI/TRATON are the immediate beneficiaries. (June 2026 — [TheTrucker.com](https://www.thetrucker.com/trucking-news/equipment-tech/self-drive-act-of-2026-would-allow-autonomous-manufacturers-to-self-certify-their-products))

- **Aurora Gen-2 hardware launching Q2 2026:** New hardware on International LT trucks enables true observer-free driverless operations. 7 commercial customers including Uber Freight, 370K driverless miles, 100% on-time, zero collisions as of Q1 close. Targeting 200+ trucks by year-end 2026. (June 2026 — [Aurora Innovation IR](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/0001828108/000182810826000050/aurora26q1shareholderlet.htm))

### 📋 Regulatory Watch

- **FMCSA ADS NPRM (new this week):** First proposed federal safety standards for ADS-equipped trucks now in public comment. This is the rulebook Uber Freight's AV carrier relationships will eventually be compliance-benchmarked against.
- **SELF DRIVE Act of 2026 (new this week):** AV self-certification bill in Congress. If passed, accelerates factory-built AV truck timelines for Aurora, Waabi, and PlusAI — directly advancing Uber Freight's AV carrier capacity strategy.
- **Section 301 tariff cliff — July 24 (7 weeks out):** Section 122 IEEPA tariffs expire; USTR Section 301 investigations across 60+ economies expected to land country-specific rates of 20–40%+ for Vietnam, Thailand, Cambodia. Binary freight import volume event — massive front-loading expected to peak mid-July, converting to domestic TL demand within 2 weeks.
- **USMCA Round 2 — June 16–17, Washington DC:** Agriculture and level-playing-field provisions on the table. Outcome shapes Mexico-corridor freight volumes for H2 2026.

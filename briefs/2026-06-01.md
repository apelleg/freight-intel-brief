## 🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-06-01
**Read time: ~4 minutes**

### ⚡ Today's Top Signal
FedEx Freight (NYSE: FDXF) begins regular-way trading today as an independent, publicly traded company — the first day in its history as a standalone entity after FedEx's spinoff completed this morning. As the largest LTL carrier in North America (~$9B revenue, ~30,000 trucks), FDXF enters the public markets with investors now empowered to push hard on pricing power, margin expansion, and market-share growth. For Uber Freight, this matters on two levels: as a carrier relationship, FDXF will now optimize pricing more aggressively to satisfy analysts; as a market signal, an independent FDXF accelerates the industry's multi-year LTL pricing cycle just as the truckload spot market is already running at multi-year highs — compressing shipper margins and tightening broker capacity access across all modes simultaneously.

### 📋 Regulatory Watch
**Section 301 tariff replacement deadline: July 24, 2026.** USTR has launched two Section 301 investigations on an "accelerated 5-month timeline" covering 60+ economies — explicitly designed to replace Section 122 (the current 10% China tariff) before it legally expires on July 24. Importers face a binary outcome: if Section 301 rates rise above current 10% levels, expect another import front-loading surge in early July into an already-tight freight market; if rates drop or hold, a Q3 volume cliff follows as pull-forward demand unwinds. This is the single largest freight volume variable for Q3 capacity and pricing planning. Additionally, the second USMCA bilateral negotiating round (agriculture + level playing field) is scheduled June 16–17 in Washington D.C. ([June 2026 — USTR](https://ustr.gov/about/policy-offices/press-office/press-releases/2026/may/united-states-and-mexico-announce-series-bilateral-negotiating-rounds-related-first-joint-review) / [Carra Globe](https://carraglobe.com/section-301-tariffs-2026/) / [Logistics Management](https://www.logisticsmgmt.com/article/u.s_and_mexico_launch_new_usmca_negotiation_rounds_ahead_of_2026_joint_review))

### 💡 CPO Lens: 3 Things Worth Discussing with Your Team

1. **FDXF Independence & LTL Product Roadmap:** With FedEx Freight now publicly traded and incentivized to maximize yield independently, UF's LTL brokerage capability faces new carrier-side commercial pressure — does UF have a multi-carrier LTL diversification strategy that reduces dependence on FDXF capacity, and is the product experience differentiated enough to absorb rate increases from a newly aggressive independent carrier?

2. **Amazon Prime Day Freight Surge (June 23–25, FBA deadline June 5):** The Prime Day freight spike arrives in 3 weeks into the tightest seasonal capacity since 2022 — is UF's shipper platform proactively surfacing this urgency to customers today, and does UF have committed carrier capacity for June 5–25 inbound Amazon FC shipments before spot rates fully reflect the demand?

3. **Section 301 Q3 Scenario Planning:** The July 24 tariff replacement cliff could trigger either another import front-loading spike (if rates rise) or a volume cliff (if rates fall) — both scenarios require different capacity posture and shipper contract strategies. What's the Q3 playbook for each outcome, and are UF's largest shipper accounts being engaged proactively on freight planning before July?

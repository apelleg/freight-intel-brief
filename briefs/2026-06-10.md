## 🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-06-10
**Read time: ~4 minutes**

### ⚡ Today's Top Signal
Uber sold 67.5 million Aurora shares at $7.10/share ($479M) on June 2 — cutting its stake from ~19% to 15.6% — and Aurora's stock (AUR) has continued sliding, closing at **$6.16 on June 9, now 13% below the block sale price** and down 25% since May 14. For Uber Freight, this is more than a financial footnote: Aurora is Uber Freight's primary autonomous trucking carrier partner (one of 7 commercial customers, 370K driverless miles in Q1), yet Uber parent is clearly monetizing its position. The critical implication: if Aurora's stock weakness impairs its ability to raise capital for the Gen-2 hardware ramp and 200-truck year-end target, Uber Freight's AV lane commitments could slip — forcing an immediate product strategy decision about whether to diversify across Waabi, Kodiak, and PlusAI or double down on Aurora.

### 🏢 Competitor Moves
- **Amazon / Prime Day freight surge:** Amazon Prime Day confirmed for June 23–26 — the earliest slot in the event's 11-year history, 3 weeks ahead of the traditional July timing. With Amazon's 80,000 dry-van trailer fleet pre-positioned and FBA inbound closed, third-party brokers face a 13-day window to capture overflow demand before Amazon's captive network absorbs it. *Implication for UF: Real-time capacity visibility and shipper proactive outreach will be directly tested; this is also a live benchmark for platform loyalty ahead of Q3 contract renewals.* **Confidence: High** ([June 8, 2026 — RMW Commerce](https://www.rmwcommerce.com/podcast-episodes/june-8-2026-amazon-prime-day-fedex-freight-anthropic-files-and-shipstation-global))

- **Uber Freight Q2 market report (June 5):** CEO Rebecca Tinucci's debut market brief flagged three converging pressures: diesel at $5.38/gal, counter-seasonal capacity tightening (supply-driven, not demand recovery), and regulatory headwinds (FMCSA enforcement + Section 301 tariff cliff July 24). Contract rates are now up **5–6% YoY excl. fuel**; trucking employment is at its lowest level in more than a decade. *Implication for UF: The self-published urgency framing positions UF as a proactive shipper advisor, but also confirms Q2 will stress-test its carrier network and pricing models simultaneously.* **Confidence: High** ([June 5, 2026 — The Trucker](https://www.thetrucker.com/trucking-news/business/uber-freight-market-pressures-converge-and-create-urgency-in-q2))

Robinson AI-driven consolidation:** CHR stock is up 46% over 52 weeks on its 30-AI-agent deployment (100% rate quotes in 32 seconds) and post-SCOTUS broker consolidation thesis; RXO is now below investment-grade (Moody's) and down 50% over the same period. *Implication for UF: Enterprise shippers are actively consolidating broker relationships to fewer, better-capitalized platforms — Uber Freight must earn its seat at that table now, before Q3 RFPs close.* **Confidence: High** ([June 2026 — FreightWaves](https://www.freightwaves.com/news/rxo-vs-c-h-robinson-the-growing-financial-divide-widens-some-more))

### 🔧 Freight Tech & AI
- **SELF DRIVE Act passes full House Energy & Commerce Committee:** H.R. 7390 cleared the full committee this week (following its 12-11 subcommittee vote), now heading to a House floor vote or inclusion in surface transportation reauthorization. Key freight-specific provision: allows AV trucks to haul **commercial freight during testing/evaluation**, preempts all state-level restrictions, and sets federal NHTSA safety standards due September 2027. This removes the multistate patchwork barrier that has confined Aurora, Waabi, and Kodiak to Texas corridors — accelerating national AV freight deployment timelines by potentially 12–18 months. ([June 2026 — Fox News](https://www.foxnews.com/politics/congress-moves-set-national-rules-self-driving-cars-overriding-states) / [ACT News](https://www.act-news.com/news/congress-formalizes-av-framework/))

- **SAP ships agentic supply chain AI into TMS:** SAP's Transportation Management platform now includes AI agents that plan, execute, and optimize shipments autonomously — carbon-aware routing to real-time exception resolution — without human input. Gartner forecasts supply chain management AI software growing from $2B in 2025 to $53B by 2030. The critical risk for Uber Freight: as enterprise TMS platforms embed agentic AI natively, the standalone case for an AI-native freight broker layer weakens unless UF demonstrates superior carrier data density and execution outcomes. ([June 2026 — SAP News Center](https://news.sap.com/2026/06/autonomous-supply-chain-why-agentic-ai-is-rewriting-the-operating-model/))

- **Drewry WCI surges 23% to $3,433/FEU:** As of June 4, Shanghai–LA spot hit $4,565/FEU (+31% WoW) and Shanghai–NY reached $5,505/FEU (+20% WoW), with only 3 blank sailings on transpacific next week vs. prior-week averages. GRI filings targeting $7,000/FEU East Coast all-in are on the table. Driver: Section 301 tariff cliff July 24 accelerating import front-loading simultaneously with Amazon Prime Day pulling peak season 3–4 weeks early. ([June 4, 2026 — Drewry/gCaptain](https://gcaptain.com/drewry-container-spot-rates-continue-climb-as-early-peak-season-gains-momentum/))

### 📋 Regulatory Watch
- **USMCA Round 2 opens this week — June 16–17, Washington DC:** The second US-Mexico bilateral round covers agriculture and "level playing field" provisions. Trade analysts now broadly expect the July 1 joint review deadline to be missed, shifting to an annual review cycle through 2036. Key freight risk: automotive/industrial rules-of-origin tightening could reshape the nearshoring freight volumes driving Mexico's 15.5% US import share — Uber Freight's highest-growth cross-border corridor. ([June 2026 — USTR](https://ustr.gov/about/policy-offices/press-office/press-releases/2026/may/united-states-and-mexico-announce-series-bilateral-negotiating-rounds-related-first-joint-review) / [Mexico Business News](https://mexicobusiness.news/trade-and-investment/news/usmca-review-unlikely-end-july-1-deadline))

- **Section 301 tariff July 7 hearing — 6-week countdown:** USTR's public hearing is July 7; implementation expected as soon as July 24. Proposed rates: 10% on Mexico, Canada, EU, Ecuador, Indonesia, Pakistan; 12.5% on 54 other economies. Any implementation at or above current IEEPA levels triggers another import front-loading wave into an already-tight spot market. ([June 2026 — Dorsey](https://www.dorsey.com/newsresources/publications/client-alerts/2026/6/new-section-301-tariffs) / [USTR](https://ustr.gov/issue-areas/enforcement/section-301-investigations/tariff-actions))

### 💡 CPO Lens: 3 Things Worth Discussing with Your Team

1. **Aurora partner-vs.-asset tension:** Uber sold $479M of Aurora shares at $7.10; the stock is now at $6.16 and declining further — given that Aurora is Uber Freight's primary AV carrier, should we be accelerating commercial agreements with Waabi, Kodiak, or PlusAI now so that the Gen-2 Aurora ramp isn't our single point of failure for autonomous lane throughput in H2?

2. **Amazon Prime Day as a platform stress test in 13 days:** With Prime Day June 23–26 and Amazon's captive 80K-trailer fleet dominating fulfillment, what is Uber Freight's specific playbook to identify and capture overflow freight demand before it lands on Amazon Freight's platform — and can we use this surge as a forcing function to demonstrate agentic load matching to shippers evaluating Q3 contract renewals?

3. **TMS-native agentic AI is now table stakes:** SAP just shipped autonomous execution agents natively into TMS, CHR has 30 agents in production, and RXO's 500K automated calls drove a Q2 guidance beat — where exactly is Uber Freight on this spectrum, and is there one specific high-volume workflow (tender automation, carrier acceptance, load tracking) where we can publish a benchmark that demonstrates measurable superiority to enterprise procurement teams this quarter?

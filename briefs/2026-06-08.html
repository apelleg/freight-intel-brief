🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-06-08

Read time: ~4 minutes

⚡ Today's Top Signal

The freight cycle just crossed from tariff-driven to fundamentally-driven: the May ISM Manufacturing PMI hit 54.0 — the highest reading since May 2022 — while the Logistics Managers' Index Transportation Prices sub-index reached an all-time record of 96.0. Coke carloads are up 28% year-over-year (a leading indicator for steel production) and durable goods orders rose 7.9% in April with transportation equipment up 21.5%, confirming that industrial demand is now layering on top of tariff pull-forward, not replacing it. For Uber Freight, this structural shift means the tight capacity environment extends through H2 2026 — the window to lock in contract repricing before it becomes adversarial is narrowing now.

📋 Regulatory Watch

Trump Customs Enforcement EO (June 3, 2026 — White House): President Trump signed an executive order directing DHS/CBP within 180 days to tighten importer-of-record eligibility (requiring minimum tangible domestic assets or higher bond levels) and within 90 days to establish a minimum 50% penalty floor for import violations. Effect on freight: raises compliance costs for marginal importers using minimal-bonding structures; freight brokers who vet importers carefully under the post-Montgomery framework gain a compliance advantage over looser competitors.

$166B IEEPA Tariff Refund Battle Escalating (June 1-4, 2026 — PBS NewsHour; Fortune): DOJ filed notice to appeal the Court of International Trade's order requiring full refund of $166B collected under struck-down IEEPA tariffs; CBP has already issued $20.6B in refunds to ~330,000 importers via its new CAPE tool. Binary outcome: if refunds proceed, shippers gain cash flow that could accelerate freight spend; if blocked, Q4 import demand remains compressed. Court fight likely extends into Q3.

USMCA: Canada Formally Requests 16-Year Extension (June 2-3, 2026 — TT News; Globe & Mail): Canadian Trade Minister Dominic LeBlanc sent formal letters to USTR Greer and Mexico's Economy Secretary on June 2 requesting a 16-year USMCA renewal; Mexico supports the extension. Trump administration wants concessions on automotive rules-of-origin and dairy market access. Second US-Mexico bilateral round June 16-17 in Washington, DC. July 1 joint review deadline widely expected to be missed, leaving cross-border freight corridors exposed to annual review cycle volatility.

## 🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-07-23
**Read time: ~4 minutes**

### ⚡ Today's Top Signal

Trimble — the software backbone for thousands of North American carriers (TMW TMS) and enterprise shippers (Transporeon) — is working with Goldman Sachs to explore the sale of its entire transportation & logistics division, which generates $550M in annual revenue and 21% of Trimble's recurring revenue base. No buyer has been named; a PE acquisition is widely expected. The timing is directly relevant to Uber Freight: TMW carriers and Transporeon shipper accounts now face vendor uncertainty just as Section 301 tariff volatility is forcing supply chain replanning. For Uber Freight's managed transportation team, this is the clearest competitive window since the Transplace acquisition — Transplace-based 4PL can be positioned as the AI-native, stable TMS alternative before any deal closes and uncertainty resolves. The product and sales motion needs to start now.

### 🗂️ TMS & Managed Transportation

- **Trimble exploring $550M T&L division sale (Goldman Sachs):** Trimble's transportation & logistics division — which includes the TMW TMS (used by thousands of truckload carriers), the Transporeon visibility and carrier management platform, and mapping products — generates $550M annually, representing 15% of Trimble revenue and 21% of annual recurring revenue. Trimble is working with Goldman Sachs on a potential sale, with no buyer confirmed as of this writing. Private equity is the most likely acquirer. For context: Transporeon competes directly with Uber Freight's Transplace in the shipper TMS/capacity procurement layer; TMW is embedded in the carrier operational stack. A PE-owned Transporeon under financial engineering pressure would be a less capable and less stable competitor — exactly when enterprise shippers are most likely to evaluate alternatives. Uber Freight's 4PL/managed transportation sales team should be proactively reaching TMW carrier partners and Transporeon shipper accounts with a Transplace value proposition now. ### 📋 Regulatory Watch

- **FMCSA 3 deregulatory rules effective July 22 (today):** Three FMCSA final rules that were published June 22 took effect at 12:01 a.m. today: (1) eliminates the requirement for CDL holders to self-report certain motor vehicle convictions to their state driver licensing agency; (2) removes the requirement to keep a physical ELD operator manual in the cab; (3) narrows when carriers must return signed roadside inspection reports to state agencies — now only when the issuing agency requests it. Net effect: reduced administrative burden on carriers and drivers, marginally supportive of capacity retention in a market where supply-side tightening has driven rate strength. (July 22, 2026 — [McFarlane Law](https://www.mcfarlanelaw.com/fmcsa-deregulatory-rules-cdl-self-reporting-2026/) — [Trucksafe](https://trucksafe.com/post/fmcsa-finalizes-more-deregulatory-rules))

### 💡 CPO Lens: 3 Things Worth Discussing with Your Team

1. **TMS competitive window — act before a buyer is named:** Trimble's Goldman-led sale process means thousands of TMW carrier customers and Transporeon shipper accounts will spend the next 90–180 days uncertain about their TMS vendor's future. This is the clearest competitive opening Transplace-based managed transportation has seen since Uber Freight's own acquisition in 2021. What does a targeted outreach campaign to Transporeon shipper accounts look like, and can the 4PL team run it in parallel with the standard enterprise pipeline?

2. **ATA tonnage vs. spot rates: what does UF's data say?** ATA June tonnage barely grew (+0.1%) while spot rates ran 55%+ above year-ago levels — confirming the rate cycle is supply-driven, not demand-driven. As a data question for the product team: does Uber Freight's internal load data corroborate the ATA picture? If the brokerage is seeing real organic demand recovery that the ATA misses, that's a competitive insight worth surfacing. If the ATA is right and we're supply-driven, Q3 rate softening after the August trough will be faster than the market expects.

3. **AI IP governance — freight enters the litigation era:** Flexport-Freightmate is the freight industry's first major AI trade secret case, and it will set precedent for how workflow automation, prompt libraries, and model training data are owned and protected. For Uber Freight's product and engineering leadership: what are current protocols for documenting AI training data provenance, proprietary workflow blueprints, and agentic system architectures across both the brokerage and Transplace platforms?

### 🔧 Freight Tech & AI

- **CHR Lean AI Engineer redefines 4PL scope** ([June 2026 — Logistics Management](https://www.logisticsmgmt.com/article/c.h_robinson_expands_ai_portfolio_with_lean_ai_engineer_for_continuous_supply_chain_optimization)): An agentic AI that replaces human supply chain analysts for continuous optimization — conducting full network assessments in 25–30 minutes (vs. 4 weeks manually), then handing off to the Lean AI Planner for execution via hundreds of interconnected agents. Early adopters have cut loads 17–81% and saved $1M+/year. The clearest signal yet that the labor-arbitrage model of managed transportation is being replaced by machine intelligence at scale.

- **Rail freight surges 7.2% YoY as shippers flee record TL rates** ([June 4, 2026 — FreightWaves](https://www.freightwaves.com/news/trucking-is-driving-double-digit-growth-for-this-rail-freight-category)): Week of May 30 — 492,795 total U.S. carloads and intermodal units; intermodal up 10% YoY to 264,449 containers and trailers; metallic ores/metals +19.5%, motor vehicles +9.1%. Shippers are actively arbitraging the 30–40% TL spot premium vs. intermodal, with the $1.20+/mile intermodal rate advantage narrowing but still significant.

- **Ocean rates spike on Amazon Prime Day pull-forward** ([June 4, 2026 — Drewry](https://www.drewry.co.uk/supply-chain-advisors/supply-chain-expertise/world-container-index-assessed-by-drewry)): Drewry World Container Index: Shanghai–LA $4,565/FEU (+31% WoW); Shanghai–NYC $5,505/FEU (+20% WoW); Shanghai–Rotterdam $3,579/FEU (+25%). Hapag-Lloyd and Maersk announced new peak season surcharges ($600–$1,000/40ft) effective June 8–10. Amazon Prime Day June 23 pulling ocean peak season 3–4 weeks early — inland domestic freight surge expected mid-June as containers clear ports.

### 📋 Regulatory Watch

**Gen Re (reinsurance) published June 2, 2026 analysis** confirming the Montgomery SCOTUS ruling leaves freight brokers with zero mandatory federal liability insurance — only a $75,000 surety bond that doesn't respond to personal injury or wrongful death judgments. The insurance market has not yet repriced this exposure; when it does, small and mid-tier brokers without systematic, documented carrier-vetting processes face structural existential cost changes. This dynamic accelerates consolidation toward large, well-capitalized brokers — including Uber Freight ([June 2, 2026 — Gen Re](https://www.genre.com/us/knowledge/publications/2026/june/the-supreme-court-opened-the-door-to-new-risks-for-freight-brokers-en)).

**SELF DRIVE Act (H.R. 7390)** advancing toward full House Energy & Commerce Committee markup — would preempt all state AV restrictions and allow AV manufacturers to self-certify without NHTSA pre-market review, potentially creating a unified national framework that accelerates Aurora and Waabi commercial deployment to a 50-state market by 2027 ([June 2026 — Congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/7390/text)).

### 💡 CPO Lens: 3 Things Worth Discussing with Your Team

1. **CHR's AI Supply Chain OS is live — what does UF build in response?** C.H. Robinson's Lean AI Engineer now autonomously *runs* shipper supply chains (not just advises), delivering $1M+ savings in early deployments by finding load consolidations in 25 minutes that humans take 4 weeks to find. As UF's new CPO: does UF have a product roadmap for continuous AI-powered network intelligence — and does UF's data moat (cross-border flows, carrier performance history, load patterns) give it an advantage CHR can't easily replicate with its domestic-heavy dataset?

2. **AV capacity at record market rates — is UF positioned to monetize the combination?** Aurora Gen-2 trucks are entering observer-free driverless service right now, at the exact moment flatbed hits all-time highs and dry van is +23% YoY. Shippers paying record rates would prefer lower-cost AV capacity if it were available and trusted. Does Uber Freight's carrier marketplace allow shippers to preference AV carriers — and if not, what would it take to build that feature before the market normalizes and the urgency window closes?

3. **SMB supply chain disruption = the largest new-shipper acquisition window in years.** 97% of SMBs are actively restructuring supply chains due to tariff volatility (Netstock 2026 Tariff Impact Report), with 75% extending planning horizons — meaning they're in active vendor evaluation mode right now. What is UF's GTM strategy to capture these newly-searching SMB shippers before CHR's Lean AI Engineer and Flexport's tariff compliance tools establish lock-in?

## 🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-06-08
**Read time: ~4 minutes**

### ⚡ Today's Top Signal
The freight cycle just crossed from tariff-driven to fundamentally-driven: the May ISM Manufacturing PMI hit 54.0 — the highest reading since May 2022 — while the Logistics Managers' Index Transportation Prices sub-index reached an all-time record of 96.0. Coke carloads are up 28% year-over-year (a leading indicator for steel production) and durable goods orders rose 7.9% in April with transportation equipment up 21.5%, confirming that industrial demand is now layering on top of tariff pull-forward, not replacing it. For Uber Freight, this structural shift means the tight capacity environment extends through H2 2026 — the window to lock in contract repricing before it becomes adversarial is narrowing now.

### 📊 Market Pulse (30 seconds)
- **Spot rates:** Dry van ~$2.80/mile (+23% YoY, within cents of 2021 all-time record); flatbed at a new all-time high of $4.32/mile (+47% YoY); reefer ~$3.10/mile (+25% YoY) (June 5-6, 2026 — DAT/Kesco Logistics Weekly)
- **Load-to-truck:** Van ~13.5 (vs. April avg 7.2), flatbed 87+, reefer ~20; tender rejections at ~17% nationally (cycle high), flatbed rejections above 38% (June 5, 2026 — LMI/SONAR)
- **Notable capacity event:** Strait of Hormuz still operating at just 7 ships/day vs. ~100 normal; Brent crude eased 2.6% to $95.18 on June 4 as US-Iran framework talks advance, but diesel nationally still ~$5.38/gal. Carrier exits accelerating: Sparhawk Trucking (178 trucks, Wisconsin) filed Chapter 11 and WARN notice May 28 with 146 drivers at risk; SP Trans (Illinois), M&L Express (Maryland), and SB Hauling (North Carolina) also filed in the same week (June 3, 2026 — Trucking Dive/WSAW)

### 🔧 Freight Tech & AI
- **ISM 54.0 + coke carloads +28% YoY signal durable industrial freight cycle:** Manufacturing-led demand now supplements tariff pull-forward as the freight upcycle driver. Flatbed's new record ($4.32/mile) reflects data center construction and manufacturing expansion, not just tariff-driven imports. AI pricing and capacity allocation models built on volatile tariff-demand patterns need recalibration for a structurally tight, industrial-led market extending through H2. (June 4-6, 2026 — FreightWaves, AAR)

- **LMI Transportation Prices at all-time record 96.0:** The May 2026 Logistics Managers' Index Transportation Prices component hit 96.0 — the highest single reading in the index's near-decade history — with Transportation Capacity down 40% since January as the Hormuz disruption, carrier bankruptcies, and regulatory exits converge. Upstream manufacturers are experiencing even tighter conditions than retailers, signaling the next wave of shipper contract stress is upstream-driven, not consumer-driven. (June 5, 2026 — LMI/FAU)

- **Amazon Prime Day June 23 freight surge imminent:** With Prime Day moved to June 23 this year (FBA deadline was May 27), transpacific spot rates spiked 31% week-over-week to $4,565/FEU on Shanghai-LA (Drewry WCI, June 4), with carriers stacking GRIs and PSS surcharges. Inland conversion of ocean containers to domestic truckload will peak roughly June 18-22 — a 5-7 day spot demand surge Uber Freight's dynamic pricing platform should be positioned to capture. (June 4-5, 2026 — Drewry, Freightos)

### 📋 Regulatory Watch
**Trump Customs Enforcement EO (June 3, 2026):** President Trump signed an executive order directing DHS/CBP within 180 days to tighten importer-of-record eligibility (requiring minimum tangible domestic assets or higher bond levels) and within 90 days to establish a minimum 50% penalty floor for import violations. Effect on freight: raises compliance costs for marginal importers using minimal-bonding structures, could reduce load volumes from that segment but increase per-load quality for compliant freight brokers. Freight brokers who vet importers carefully under the post-Montgomery framework gain a compliance advantage.

**$166B IEEPA Tariff Refund Battle Escalating (June 1-4, 2026):** DOJ filed notice to appeal the Court of International Trade's order requiring full refund of $166B collected under struck-down IEEPA tariffs; CBP has already issued $20.6B in refunds to ~330,000 importers via its new CAPE tool. Binary outcome: if refunds proceed, shippers gain cash flow that could accelerate freight spend; if blocked, Q4 import demand remains compressed. The court fight could extend into Q3.

**USMCA: Canada Formally Requests 16-Year Extension (June 2-3, 2026):** Canadian Trade Minister Dominic LeBlanc sent formal letters to USTR Greer and Mexico's Economy Secretary requesting a 16-year USMCA renewal; Mexico supports the extension. Trump administration wants concessions on automotive rules-of-origin and dairy market access. Second US-Mexico bilateral round June 16-17 in Washington, DC. July 1 joint review deadline widely expected to be missed, leaving cross-border freight corridors exposed to annual review cycle volatility.

### 💡 CPO Lens: 3 Things Worth Discussing with Your Team

1. **Contract Repricing Window Is Now:** ISM Manufacturing PMI 54.0 and LMI Transportation Prices at record 96.0 confirm the freight cycle has structural legs through H2. Uber Freight's Q2 update already showed first-tender acceptance at 82% and spot volumes up 44% YoY. Should we be proactively approaching shipper contract renegotiations now — before routing guide stress forces adversarial conversations in Q3 — and how do we do this in a way that builds long-term customer trust in Amir's first 90 days rather than just extracting rate?

2. **Responding to CHR's AI Consolidation Narrative:** C.H. Robinson is publicly framing itself as the AI-powered consolidator that absorbs market share as smaller brokers exit. Uber Freight has genuine AI assets — Waabi partnership, agentic carrier dispatch, market intelligence platform — but has been quieter in public messaging. What is Uber Freight's AI story for enterprise accounts entering H2 sales cycles, and how do we differentiate from CHR's "AI runs your supply chain for you" pitch?

3. **Aurora Stake Sale and AV Portfolio Strategy:** Uber parent sold 14% of its Aurora position in a single block trade while Uber Freight remains an Aurora commercial customer. Is this a financial portfolio decision or a signal about conviction in Aurora's commercial ramp velocity? Should Uber Freight explicitly expand its AV partnership footprint (Waabi, Kodiak, Volvo AV/DSV) to hedge single-partner risk — and what does Amir want UF's AV partnership philosophy to be as new CPO?

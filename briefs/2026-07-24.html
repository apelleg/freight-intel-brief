🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-07-24

Read time: ~4 minutes

⚡ Today's Top Signal

Section 301 forced-labor tariffs went live at 12:01 am ET today as the temporary Section 122 10% global tariff expired by operation of law — transitioning U.S. trade policy from a time-limited emergency measure to a permanent statutory framework. Unlike Section 122 (which carried a 150-day statutory cap), Section 301 has no rate ceiling and no fixed expiration date: 10% tariffs on most of the 60 affected trading partners, and 12.5% on China and select others, are now the indefinite baseline. USMCA-compliant goods are exempted — making Uber Freight's US-Mexico cross-border corridor structurally more valuable overnight. A vessel-loading grace period applies: goods loaded before today and entered for consumption before July 28 are spared. For Uber Freight's managed transportation business, this is the moment shipper routing strategies permanently embed tariff complexity — and the Transplace TMS needs to reflect that.

📋 Regulatory Watch

  • Section 301 forced-labor tariffs live today (July 24): USTR published final action July 23; 10%–12.5% tariffs on goods from 60 trading partners took effect at 12:01 am EDT today as Section 122 expired. No rate cap, no statutory expiration — effectively permanent. USMCA-compliant goods are exempt. Goods loaded on a vessel before today and entered for consumption before July 28 qualify for the grace period. Exempted products include oil, gas, and fertilizer. China faces the 12.5% rate; EU, Mexico, and 57 other economies face 10%. (Supply Chain Dive · Transport Topics, July 23–24, 2026)
  • USMCA Round 3 ongoing: US and Mexico are mid-way through their third bilateral negotiating round in Mexico City this week. Canada remains excluded while facing 50% Section 338 tariffs (imposed July 20). Central issues: automotive rules of origin, Chinese content embedded in Mexico supply chains, steel/aluminum. USMCA remains in force through 2036 under the annual review mechanism triggered July 1. (US News, July 21–24, 2026)

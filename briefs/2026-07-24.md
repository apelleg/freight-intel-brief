## 🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-07-24
**Read time: ~4 minutes**

### ⚡ Today's Top Signal

Section 301 forced-labor tariffs went live at 12:01 am ET today as the temporary Section 122 10% global tariff expired by operation of law — transitioning U.S. trade policy from a time-limited emergency measure to a permanent statutory framework. Unlike Section 122 (which carried a 150-day statutory cap), Section 301 has no rate ceiling and no fixed expiration date: 10% tariffs on most of the 60 affected trading partners, and 12.5% on China and select others, are now the indefinite baseline. USMCA-compliant goods are exempted — making Uber Freight's US-Mexico cross-border corridor structurally more valuable overnight. A vessel-loading grace period applies: goods loaded before today and entered for consumption before July 28 are spared. For Uber Freight's managed transportation business, this is the moment shipper routing strategies permanently embed tariff complexity — and the Transplace TMS needs to reflect that.

### 📋 Regulatory Watch

- **Section 301 forced-labor tariffs live today (July 24):** USTR published final action July 23; 10%–12.5% tariffs on goods from 60 trading partners took effect at 12:01 am EDT today as Section 122 expired. No rate cap, no statutory expiration — effectively permanent. USMCA-compliant goods are exempt. Goods loaded on a vessel before today and entered for consumption before July 28 qualify for the grace period. Exempted products include oil, gas, and fertilizer. China faces the 12.5% rate; EU, Mexico, and 57 other economies face 10%. ([Supply Chain Dive](https://www.supplychaindive.com/news/us-imposes-tariffs-over-forced-labor-before-global-duty-ends/825951/) · [USTR](https://ustr.gov/about/policy-offices/press-office/press-releases/2026/june/ustr-makes-findings-and-proposes-action-60-section-301-investigations-relating-failures-take-action) · [Transport Topics](https://www.ttnews.com/articles/us-shifts-section-301-tariffs), July 23–24, 2026)

- **USMCA Round 3 ongoing:** US and Mexico are mid-way through their third bilateral negotiating round in Mexico City this week. Canada remains excluded while facing 50% Section 338 tariffs (imposed July 20). Central issues: automotive rules of origin, Chinese content embedded in Mexico supply chains, steel/aluminum. USMCA remains in force through 2036 under the annual review mechanism triggered July 1. ([US News](https://www.usnews.com/news/world/articles/2026-07-21/us-mexico-resume-usmca-trade-talks-as-trump-hits-canada-with-new-tariffs), July 21–24, 2026)

### 💡 CPO Lens: 3 Things Worth Discussing with Your Team

1. **Section 301 + USMCA exemption = Mexico corridor strategic moat:** With Section 301 now the permanent tariff baseline, USMCA-compliant goods flowing through the US-Mexico corridor are structurally advantaged — exempt from the new duties that apply to the same goods sourced from China or EU. Uber Freight has been building cross-border capability as a growth lever; this is the moment to translate that into an enterprise positioning story. How should the 4PL team be reframing the Mexico corridor to shippers who are actively redesigning their sourcing in response to today's tariff shift?

2. **Transplace TMS embedded brokerage — where does UF stand?** DAT just embedded its carrier-matching engine inside Tai TMS, and SONAR embedded inside Blue Yonder — two days in a row. The trend is clear: the TMS transaction layer is where freight matching is heading, not standalone tools. Does Transplace's roadmap include an API or embedded brokerage product that can sit inside third-party enterprise TMS workflows? If not, Transplace risks being a closed system while competitors build the open infrastructure layer.

3. **Q2 earnings week — set the internal baseline:** CH Robinson (July 29), Aurora (July 29), XPO (July 30), and eventually RXO (August 6) will all report next week. This is the industry benchmark moment for what AI-driven brokerage profitability, autonomous freight commercialization, and LTL margin discipline look like in this cycle. Before the calls, what does Uber Freight's own Q2 performance look like across brokerage gross margin, Transplace load volume, and AI automation coverage? Having those numbers ready to contextualize against public peers' calls would be high value.

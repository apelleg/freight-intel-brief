### 🔧 Freight Tech & AI

- **LSP44 as embedded freight infrastructure:** The new LSP44 entity provides AI agent and API infrastructure that logistics providers embed directly into their own tech stacks — it is not a shipper-facing application but an infrastructure layer. Both project44 and LSP44 share one underlying AI-native carrier network and data graph, which is the durable competitive asset. Any 3PL or broker that runs LSP44 gets AI-agent connectivity into the world's largest carrier API network without building it themselves. This is the logistics equivalent of AWS for freight intermediaries. (July 14, 2026 — project44 / DC Velocity / Logistics Management)

- **Aurora Gen-2 hardware confirmed launched:** International LT Series trucks now operating in observerless mode (no partner-requested safety observer required) following the Q2 Gen-2 hardware deployment — the first generation enabling full commercial driverless operations at scale. Key specs: 1M-mile hardware lifespan (~2× Gen-1), 1,000m FirstLight lidar range (2×), 50% cost-per-truck reduction. Fleet scaling toward 200+ driverless trucks by year-end 2026. Full Q2 fleet and revenue data releases July 29. (Aurora Q1 2026 earnings guidance — confirmed Q2 launch per Aurora IR roadmap)

### 🗂️ TMS & Managed Transportation

**LSP44 and the Transplace integration question:** project44's creation of LSP44 as a standalone, profitable AI infrastructure company targeting 3PLs and freight brokers directly raises a platform architecture question for Uber Freight's Transplace-based 4PL operation. If LSP44 becomes the de facto carrier-API and agent spine that major logistics providers run on — and 9 of the world's 10 largest already do — then Transplace's carrier connectivity layer may either need to integrate with LSP44 (becoming a customer) or maintain independent infrastructure to avoid dependency. This is a live integration decision, not a hypothetical: LSP44 is already embedded in the largest competitors in the 4PL space. (July 14, 2026 — project44)

**LTL GRI wave reshaping 4PL mode economics:** Saia (+7.1%, July 6) follows ABF (+5.9%, June 22) and Old Dominion (+4.9% earlier in the cycle) in sequentially raising LTL rates ahead of typical cadence. The driver is TL overflow: as truckload capacity tightens and spot TL rates run at $2.97+/mi, shippers are routing overflow freight into LTL — filling LTL networks and enabling carriers to raise rates. For Transplace-managed accounts, this creates a dynamic where the optimal mode choice is shifting weekly. A TMS feature that automatically surfaces the break-even TL/LTL crossover point by lane, weight class, and current rate — updated daily — would be a concrete managed transportation differentiator against both traditional 4PLs and digital brokerages. (July 2026 — Transport Topics / Distribution Strategy Group)

### 📋 Regulatory Watch

- **Section 301 tariffs — T-8 days (July 24):** USTR's 10–12.5% tariff framework covering 60+ countries (representing 99.4% of U.S. imports by value) goes live July 24 with zero grandfathering — every CBP entry after midnight July 23 is subject to new rates regardless of when goods shipped. No extension or phase-in has been announced. The Journal of Commerce warns compliance uncertainty is already "freezing Q3 freight volume decisions" even as the final 8-day front-loading window adds near-term demand pressure to the carrier market. (July 2026 — USTR / Journal of Commerce)

Mexico's Economy Minister Ebrard reports open issues have dropped from 54 to 14, with remaining sticking points on automotive rules of origin, agriculture, and economic security. Canada continues to be excluded from bilateral negotiations. A deal framework could stabilize cross-border freight conditions for Uber Freight's Mexico corridor; continued impasse pushes uncertainty into an ongoing annual review cycle. (July 2026 — USTR / Holland & Knight / Mexico Business News)

### 💡 CPO Lens: 3 Things Worth Discussing with Your Team

1. **Dual-shock scenario planning for 4PL clients:** With Hormuz fuel re-escalation AND Section 301 landing on the same 8-day horizon, enterprise shippers in the Transplace book face simultaneous cost shocks on transportation (diesel) and goods (tariffs). Has the 4PL team reached out proactively to strategic accounts with a scenario model — showing total landed cost impact by SKU origin, tariff classification, mode, and fuel-price scenario — before July 24? This is the moment when managed transportation earns its premium over commodity brokerage.

2. **LSP44 competitive positioning:** project44 just launched a dedicated AI infrastructure product for 3PLs and brokers — already embedded in 9 of the world's 10 largest LSPs. Do we have a clear answer to: is LSP44 a carrier visibility layer we should integrate with, or is building equivalent infrastructure independently a strategic differentiator for Uber Freight's platform? A product-engineering session this week should map our carrier API architecture against what LSP44 actually delivers, and identify where the boundaries are between complementary and competitive.

3. **TL/LTL mode optimization as a 4PL product feature:** Saia and ABF are raising LTL GRIs aggressively as TL overflow fills their networks — yet TL spot is simultaneously at $2.97+/mi records. For shippers with mixed-weight profiles, the optimal mode selection is shifting day-to-day. Does the Transplace TMS automatically surface the break-even TL/LTL crossover point by lane and shipment weight class in real time? If not, this is a near-term product build that could become a named differentiator in our managed transportation value proposition.

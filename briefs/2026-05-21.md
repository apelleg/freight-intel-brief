## 🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-05-21
**Read time: ~4 minutes**

### ⚡ Today's Top Signal

New analysis from supply chain software firm Infios, published May 19–20, found that tariff stacking has pushed effective duty rates to 20–80% on many import lines — and fundamentally rewired how shippers execute logistics. After analyzing over one million U.S. customs entries, Infios found HTS classification complexity nearly doubled (6 to 11.6 sequences per entry), bonded warehouse usage climbed from 10% to 16–18% of all entries, and air freight permanently gained 12 percentage points of modal share from ocean. For Uber Freight, this is not a tariff story — it is a freight execution product story. Shippers are now making mode selection, routing, and warehousing decisions through a live tariff lens on every shipment, and the TMS platform that integrates this tradeoff in real time wins the enterprise relationship.

Shippers now need platforms that dynamically integrate tariff compliance with mode selection and routing decisions.

### 💡 CPO Lens: 3 Things Worth Discussing with Your Team

1. **Tariff-Aware Freight Execution as a Platform Wedge:** The Infios data shows shippers are running 11.6-step HTS classification sequences per customs entry — well beyond manual capacity. Does Uber Freight's TMS surface tariff-aware mode tradeoffs, bonded warehouse options, and duty deferral pathways at the moment of freight booking? This is the moment to build this wedge before CHR or Flexport do.

2. **AI Responsiveness as a Competitive Benchmark:** CHR publicly disclosed 100% rate quote response in 32 seconds as a competitive differentiator. What is Uber Freight's current AI-to-shipper response metric on rate quotes, and is this being measured and communicated externally? As CHR closes the digital gap, UF needs to either leapfrog this metric or shift positioning to a dimension incumbents can't replicate.

3. **Midwest AV Corridor Onboarding:** Einride's Ohio deployment is the first signal that AV freight is expanding into the industrial Midwest. Does Uber Freight's platform support carrier-agnostic AV onboarding at scale — or would each new AV carrier in each new corridor require individual integration work? A reusable AV carrier onboarding layer becomes a strategic asset as the geographic map expands.

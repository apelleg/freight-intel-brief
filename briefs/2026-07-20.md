## 🚛 Uber Freight Intel Brief — 2026-07-20
**Read time: ~4 minutes**

### ⚡ Today's Top Signal

U.S. container imports are on track to hit an all-time record **2.47M TEU in July** — eclipsing the May 2022 pandemic peak — as shippers front-load ahead of Section 301 tariffs implementing in four days (July 24). The reversal is already locked in: NRF projects August imports will drop to 2.22M TEU (-4.5% YoY), a roughly 10% month-over-month collapse in inbound volume. For Uber Freight, this is the demand cliff that matters most: the brokerage side faces a sudden spot-demand evaporation while 4PL/managed transportation clients will need TMS-driven mode optimization and carrier flexibility tools to absorb the whiplash. The record-import surge is also what has kept spot rates 40-50% above year-ago levels — when it ends, the rate narrative shifts overnight.

### 📋 Regulatory Watch

- **Section 301 tariffs: T-4 days** (effective July 24, 2026): New duties of 10-12.5% on 99.4% of U.S. imports by value (by trading partner count) replace the expiring Section 122 10% global tariff (statutory sunset July 24). Some country-specific Section 301 duties already effective July 22 — Brazil at 25%, for example. Duty rate is set by entry date; goods on the water can enter in the gap if timed after the lapse. No extension legislation pending. NRF projects July imports will hit an all-time record 2.47M TEU driven by front-loading, then fall to 2.22M TEU in August (-4.5% YoY). (<a href="https://ustr.gov/about/policy-offices/press-office/press-releases/2026/june/ustr-makes-findings-and-proposes-action-60-section-301-investigations-relating-failures-take-action">June 2026 — USTR</a>; <a href="https://nrf.com/media-center/press-releases/import-cargo-expected-to-set-new-record-ahead-of-potential-august-tariffs">July 2026 — NRF</a>)

- **FMCSA three deregulatory rules effective July 22** (2 days): Removes CDL self-reporting requirement, rescinds the physical in-cab ELD manual, revises roadside inspection corrected-report procedures. (<a href="https://www.mcfarlanelaw.com/fmcsa-deregulatory-rules-cdl-self-reporting-2026/">July 2026 — McFarlane Law</a>)

- **USMCA Round 3 begins July 21** (tomorrow): US-Mexico bilateral talks in Mexico City. Key US demands: automotive rules of origin raised to 82% (from 75%), plus a new 50% US-specific content requirement, plus restrictions on Chinese components routed through Mexico. Open issues reduced from 54 to 14. Canada excluded. U.S. chose annual review over 16-year renewal at July 1 deadline — cross-border uncertainty locked in through at least 2027. (<a href="https://ustr.gov/about/policy-offices/press-office/press-releases/2026/july/united-states-and-mexico-convene-mexico-city-third-bilateral-negotiating-round-related-joint-review">July 2026 — USTR</a>; <a href="https://www.riotimesonline.com/us-mexico-usmca-third-round-autos-china-2026/">Rio Times Online</a>)

### 💡 CPO Lens: 3 Things Worth Discussing with Your Team

1. **The demand cliff is 4 days away — are our shipper clients ready?** NRF forecasts a 10% month-over-month import volume drop from July to August, right as Section 301 tariffs land. On the 4PL side, does our Transplace TMS have the forecasting tools to help managed transportation clients right-size carrier commitments and avoid over-contracted capacity that suddenly looks expensive in August? On the brokerage side, which spot capacity corridors are most exposed to a demand air pocket?

2. **DocAI win vs. CHR's AI narrative — how do we turn one into a sales story?** Uber Freight just won a major industry AI award, and CHR is dominating the AI-in-4PL story with Lean AI Engineer. Can we build a rapid case study package around DocAI and our existing AI integrations to put in front of enterprise shippers? The competitive window is July-August, before CHR's Q2 call shapes the analyst narrative again.

3. **USMCA Round 3 starts tomorrow — have we modeled the cross-border freight impact?** If automotive rules of origin rise to 82% regional + 50% US-specific, nearshoring economics in Mexico change significantly. Uber Freight has meaningful cross-border volume in automotive and industrial corridors. Do we have a view on which shipper clients are most exposed, and should we be proactively offering USMCA scenario planning as a value-add service through the 4PL side?
